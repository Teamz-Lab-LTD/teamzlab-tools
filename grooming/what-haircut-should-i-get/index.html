How the Haircut Quiz Works

This quiz analyzes your face shape, hair texture, maintenance preference, and personal style to recommend haircuts that actually suit you. Unlike generic "top 10 haircuts" lists, this tool considers your unique combination of features. Face shape is the most important factor — an oval face suits almost any cut, while a round face benefits from styles that add height and angles. Hair texture matters too: curly hair has different ideal lengths and layers than straight hair.

Best Haircuts by Face Shape

Oval face: The most versatile shape. Crew cuts, quiffs, layered bobs, and curtain bangs all work. Avoid styles that cover the forehead entirely, as this hides your balanced proportions.

Round face: Add height and angles. Pompadours, faux hawks, long layers, and side-swept bangs elongate the face. Avoid blunt bobs or very short buzz cuts that emphasize roundness.

Square face: Soften strong angles with textured crops, side parts, soft layers, or waves. Undercuts and slicked-back styles also work well by balancing the jawline.

Heart face: Balance a wider forehead with chin-length bobs, side-swept fringe, textured layers, or medium-length styles with volume at the bottom.

Oblong face: Add width, not height. Bangs, bobs, layered cuts, and side parts work best. Avoid very long straight hair or tall pompadours that lengthen further.

Hair Texture and Haircut Selection

Your hair texture dramatically affects how a cut looks in practice. Straight hair shows clean lines in bobs and blunt cuts. Wavy hair adds natural volume, making textured crops and layered styles look effortless. Curly hair benefits from layers that reduce bulk and define curls. Coarse or thick hair needs thinning or texturizing to avoid a heavy, blocky appearance. Always consider how much styling time you have — a low-maintenance person should avoid cuts that require daily blow-drying or product.

Trending Haircuts in 2026

The taper fade continues to dominate men's hair, with the low taper fade being the most requested cut at barbershops worldwide. For women, the butterfly cut and modern shag remain popular for their low-maintenance, lived-in texture. Curtain bangs work across genders and face shapes. The "quiet luxury" trend favors clean, polished cuts — think classic bobs and neat side parts over bold undercuts.
