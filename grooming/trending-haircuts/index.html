Biggest Haircut Trends of 2026

2026 is defined by two opposing movements: the "quiet luxury" trend favoring clean, polished cuts, and the "messy maximalism" trend embracing texture and volume. For men, the low taper fade remains the #1 most requested cut globally, but the textured crop and modern mullet are gaining ground fast. For women, the butterfly cut and modern shag continue their dominance, while the blunt bob is making a strong comeback as the anti-layers movement grows.

Men's Hair Trends 2026

The low taper fade has been the dominant men's cut for three years running, and 2026 shows no signs of slowing down. The textured crop is the second most requested, valued for its low-maintenance appeal. Curtain bangs for men hit peak popularity in early 2026, driven by social media. The modern mullet — short on top and sides, party in the back — has evolved from ironic to genuinely stylish, with the "wolf mullet" being the breakout variation. Slicked-back styles are returning for formal occasions, with a matte rather than wet finish.

Women's Hair Trends 2026

The butterfly cut remains the #1 women's trend — dramatic face-framing layers that flip outward create volume and movement without heat styling. The modern shag is the #2 trend, favored for its effortless, low-maintenance texture. Blunt bobs are trending again as the pendulum swings from layered to clean-cut. For color, copper continues to dominate, with "cowboy copper" and "strawberry bronze" being the most searched variations. Curtain bangs are still going strong, working across almost every face shape and hair type.

How to Ask for a Trending Haircut

Bring a reference photo — it is worth more than 100 words of description. Tell your stylist your hair type and daily styling routine so they can adapt the trend to your reality. Ask about maintenance: "How often will I need to come back?" and "Can I style this without a blow dryer?" The best haircuts are trends adapted to your features, not copied exactly from a photo. A good stylist will adjust the trend to flatter your face shape and work with your hair texture.
