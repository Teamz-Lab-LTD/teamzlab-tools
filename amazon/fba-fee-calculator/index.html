How Amazon FBA Fees Are Calculated

Amazon charges three main types of fees for Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA) sellers. The referral fee is a percentage of the selling price that varies by product category, ranging from 5% to 45%. The FBA fulfillment fee covers picking, packing, and shipping your product to the customer, determined by the item's size tier and weight. Monthly storage fees are charged per cubic foot of warehouse space your inventory occupies, with higher rates during peak season (October through December). Understanding these three cost components is essential for accurate profit calculations and competitive product pricing.

This FBA fee calculator computes all three fee types automatically based on your product details. It determines the correct size tier from your dimensions and weight, applies the category-specific referral fee rate, and estimates monthly storage costs to give you a complete picture of your Amazon selling costs.

Amazon FBA Size Tiers Explained

Amazon classifies every FBA product into a size tier that determines the fulfillment fee. Small Standard items must weigh 1 lb or less, with dimensions not exceeding 15 x 12 x 0.75 inches. Large Standard items can weigh up to 20 lbs with maximum dimensions of 18 x 14 x 8 inches. Products exceeding Large Standard dimensions or weighing over 20 lbs fall into Large Bulky, while items over 150 lbs or with any single dimension over 108 inches are classified as Extra-Large. Moving from one size tier to the next significantly increases fulfillment costs, so product design and packaging decisions directly impact your margins.

The fulfillment fee for a Small Standard item starts at $3.22 for items 15 oz or less. Large Standard items start at $3.86 for products 0.75 lb or less, with incremental charges as weight increases. Large Bulky items start at $9.73, and Extra-Large items begin at $26.33. For heavier items, per-pound surcharges apply above the first pound, making weight optimization a critical factor in maintaining healthy profit margins.

Referral Fees by Product Category

Each Amazon product category carries a specific referral fee percentage. Most categories charge 15% of the selling price, including Home, Kitchen, Toys, Sports, and Office Products. Electronics, Computers, and Camera products benefit from a lower 8% rate. Clothing and Accessories carry a 17% fee, while Jewelry charges 20% on the first $250 and 5% above that. Some categories have tiered rates based on price — Baby Products and Beauty charge 8% on items under $10 and 15% above. The minimum referral fee is $0.30 per item, which applies when the percentage-based fee would be lower.

Understanding your category's referral fee structure is crucial for product selection and pricing strategy. Categories with lower referral fees like Electronics (8%) allow for thinner margins on competitive products, while high-fee categories like Amazon Device Accessories (45%) require significantly higher markup to remain profitable.

Tips to Reduce Your Amazon FBA Fees

Optimize your product packaging to stay within a smaller size tier. Even a slight reduction in dimensions or weight can drop your item from Large Standard to Small Standard, saving over $0.50 per unit. Consider using poly bags instead of boxes where possible. Source lighter materials and eliminate unnecessary packaging components. For products near a weight threshold, every ounce matters — a product at 1.1 lbs pays significantly more than one at 0.95 lbs.

Manage inventory levels to minimize storage fees, especially during peak season when rates nearly triple. Use Amazon's Inventory Performance Index (IPI) to track aged inventory and plan promotions to move slow-selling stock before long-term storage fees apply. Multi-channel fulfillment and third-party logistics can also reduce costs for certain product types.
