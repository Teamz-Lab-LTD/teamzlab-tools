What Are Amazon Referral Fees?

Amazon referral fees are a percentage-based commission that Amazon charges on every item sold through its marketplace. The fee is calculated based on the total selling price, which includes the item price plus any shipping or gift wrap charges billed to the customer. Referral fees are the primary way Amazon earns revenue from third-party sellers and apply regardless of whether you use FBA or Merchant Fulfilled shipping.

The referral fee rate varies by product category, ranging from as low as 5% for high-value jewelry to as high as 45% for Amazon Device Accessories. Most categories fall in the 8% to 17% range, with 15% being the most common rate across categories like Home, Kitchen, Toys, Sports, and Office Products. Understanding your category's referral fee is essential for accurate pricing and profit calculations.

How Tiered Referral Fees Work

Several Amazon categories use a tiered referral fee structure where the rate changes based on the item's selling price. For example, Baby Products charges 8% on items priced at $10 or below and 15% on items above $10. Beauty, Health, and Grocery follow similar tiered models. This structure means lower-priced items in these categories benefit from reduced rates, while higher-priced items pay the standard percentage.

Jewelry and Watches use a different tiered model where the rate applies to portions of the price. Jewelry charges 20% on the first $250 of the selling price and 5% on any amount above $250. Watches charge 16% up to $1,500 and 3% above that threshold. These split-rate structures significantly benefit sellers of high-value items, as the effective rate decreases as the price increases. Electronics Accessories also uses an inverted tier: 15% for items at $100 or below and 8% for items above $100.

Minimum Referral Fee Explained

Amazon applies a minimum referral fee of $0.30 per item for most categories. If the percentage-based referral fee calculation results in an amount less than $0.30, Amazon charges $0.30 instead. This primarily impacts very low-priced items. For instance, a $1 item in the Electronics category (8% rate) would have a calculated fee of $0.08, but Amazon would charge the $0.30 minimum instead, making the effective rate 30%.

The minimum fee means that extremely low-priced items carry disproportionately high effective referral rates. Sellers of low-cost items should factor this minimum into their pricing strategy to maintain healthy margins. Bundling multiple low-cost items into a single listing can help spread the minimum fee across more units.

Tips to Manage Amazon Referral Fees

While referral fees are non-negotiable, sellers can optimize around them. Choose product categories strategically — Electronics at 8% is far more margin-friendly than Clothing at 17% for similar price points. For tiered categories, pricing just above or below a threshold can significantly change your fee. Consider whether your product could legitimately fit into a lower-fee category by adjusting its listing classification.

Bundle products to increase the average selling price, which dilutes the impact of the minimum referral fee on low-priced items. Calculate your all-in costs including referral fees, FBA fees, product cost, and shipping before setting your retail price. Use this calculator regularly when evaluating new products to ensure healthy margins before committing to inventory purchases.
