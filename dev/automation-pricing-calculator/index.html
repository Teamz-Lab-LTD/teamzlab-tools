How the Automation Pricing Calculator Works

This free calculator compares the monthly cost of running your automation workflows on three leading platforms: n8n, Zapier, and Make (formerly Integromat). Each platform uses a different billing model, making direct comparisons difficult without a calculator like this.

How Each Platform Counts Usage

Zapier charges per task — each step in a workflow counts as one task. A 5-step workflow running 1,000 times = 5,000 tasks. Make charges per operation, similar to tasks. n8n charges per execution — a 5-step workflow running 1,000 times = 1,000 executions regardless of steps. This can mean a 5-20x cost difference at scale.

n8n vs Zapier vs Make: Key Differences

Zapier leads with 6,000+ integrations and is the easiest to learn. Make offers 1,500+ integrations with deeper API access at lower prices. n8n has 1,000+ integrations plus the unique advantage of self-hosting — eliminating monthly fees entirely if you have the technical skills to maintain a server.

When to Choose Each Platform

Choose Zapier if you need maximum app compatibility and simplicity. Choose Make if you want powerful features at a lower price point. Choose n8n if you want full control, self-hosting capability, and the lowest long-term cost for high-volume automations.

Self-Hosting n8n: Hidden Costs

While n8n is free to self-host, you still need a server. A basic VPS costs $5-20 per month depending on your provider (Hetzner, DigitalOcean, AWS). Factor in your time for maintenance, updates, and monitoring. For small teams, n8n Cloud at $20/month may be more cost-effective than self-hosting.

Automation Pricing Calculator for Teams

For growing teams, automation costs can spiral quickly. A marketing team running 50 workflows with 10 steps each at 5,000 monthly runs could pay $0 on self-hosted n8n, $99-599 on Make, or $299-799 on Zapier depending on the plan. Use this automation pricing calculator to forecast costs before committing to a platform, and revisit when your usage grows to ensure you are still on the most cost-effective option.
