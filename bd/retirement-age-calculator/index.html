How Retirement Age Works in Bangladesh

The retirement age calculator Bangladesh applies the official sector rules to your date of birth to find the exact day you reach superannuation. In Bangladesh, the age of retirement is fixed by service rules, labour law, or board resolutions depending on where you work. The standard public sector retirement age is 59 years, private sector contracts usually stop at 60, and public university professors can serve until 65. This tool lets you compare all four sectors and shows the exact date you reach retirement so you can plan your pension, gratuity, and provident fund withdrawals.

Government vs Private Sector Rules

Government employees in Bangladesh retire at 59 years under the Public Servants (Retirement) Act, with a few specialised positions retiring at 60. Autonomous bodies and state-owned enterprises usually follow the same 59-year rule but some adopt 60. Private sector companies typically set retirement at 60 in the appointment letter, though many firms allow extensions on a contractual basis. Banks — both state-owned like Sonali, Janata, Agrani, and Rupali, and private commercial banks — use 60 as the superannuation age. Public university teachers benefit from an extended tenure to 65 years, recognising the academic career path.

Planning for Your Retirement Date

Knowing your exact retirement date helps you plan three critical things: your final salary drawdown, your gratuity payout, and the transition of health coverage. Enter your date of birth and joining date to see how many service years you will complete — this number directly affects your gratuity (government uses last basic × service years × ratio) and your pension entitlement. If you are eligible for voluntary retirement (age 55+ with 25 years of service), the tool will show that window too, so you can weigh early retirement against continuing until mandatory superannuation.

Gratuity and Pension Entitlements

For government employees, gratuity is calculated using the formula: last drawn basic pay × total service years × pension ratio (typically 90% for full service). Monthly pension equals basic × length-of-service factor, with a portion commuted for a lump sum. Private sector gratuity under the Bangladesh Labour Act is generally 30 days of basic wage per year of service for employees with 10+ years of continuous service. Bank employees usually follow their Staff Service Rules which match or exceed labour law minimums. Keep your appointment letter, pay fixation orders, and last pay certificate ready — these are the documents your HR will need to process your final settlement on the retirement date this calculator shows.
