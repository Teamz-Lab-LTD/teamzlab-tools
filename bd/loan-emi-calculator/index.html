How EMI Calculation Works

An Equated Monthly Installment (EMI) is the fixed payment a borrower makes to a lender on a specified date each month. EMI payments cover both principal and interest, ensuring the loan is fully repaid by the end of the tenure. The EMI amount depends on three factors: the loan principal, the annual interest rate, and the loan tenure in months. Bangladesh Bank regulates lending rates, and most banks follow the single-digit interest rate cap for priority sectors introduced in 2020, though personal loans and credit cards can carry higher rates.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)n / ((1 + r)n − 1)

Where:

  • P = Loan Principal Amount (in ৳)
  • r = Monthly Interest Rate = Annual Rate / 12 / 100
  • n = Total Monthly Installments = Years × 12
  • Total Payment = EMI × n
  • Total Interest = Total Payment − Principal

Loan Types in Bangladesh

Home loans in Bangladesh typically range from 5 to 25 years with interest rates between 9% and 10%. Banks like Islami Bank offer Shariah-compliant home financing under musharaka or diminishing musharaka arrangements where the rate is described as a profit rate rather than interest. Car loans usually span 3 to 7 years at rates between 9% and 12%, with the vehicle serving as collateral. Personal loans are unsecured and carry higher rates, typically 13% to 18%, with shorter tenures of 1 to 5 years. Education loans are emerging in Bangladesh, offered by select banks at concessional rates to fund higher education domestically or abroad.

When choosing a loan, consider the total cost of borrowing rather than just the monthly EMI. A longer tenure reduces your monthly payment but significantly increases the total interest paid. Compare Bank Interest Rates

Bangladesh has a diverse banking landscape with conventional banks, Islamic banks, and foreign banks each offering different rate structures. Islami Bank Bangladesh, the largest Islamic bank, uses profit-sharing models that effectively charge around 9% for most loan types. BRAC Bank and Dutch-Bangla Bank are popular for competitive rates on home and car loans. Eastern Bank (EBL) and City Bank cater to salaried professionals with quick processing. Standard Chartered, as a multinational bank, offers premium services but typically at slightly higher rates. Always compare the Annual Percentage Rate (APR) and processing fees across banks before committing to a loan.

Tips to Get Lower EMI

To reduce your monthly EMI burden, start by negotiating the interest rate with your bank, especially if you have a strong credit history and stable income. Making a larger down payment reduces the principal and therefore the EMI. Choose a longer tenure to spread payments, but be aware of the increased total interest cost. Consider prepaying when you receive bonuses or windfalls, as partial prepayments reduce the outstanding principal. Compare offers from at least three banks before deciding. Maintaining a good credit score with the Credit Information Bureau (CIB) of Bangladesh Bank helps you qualify for preferential rates.
