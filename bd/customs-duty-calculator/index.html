How Bangladesh Customs Duty Works

When you import goods into Bangladesh, the National Board of Revenue (NBR) levies multiple taxes and duties at the port of entry. The primary components are Customs Duty (CD), Supplementary Duty (SD), Value Added Tax (VAT), Regulatory Duty (RD), and Advance Income Tax (AIT). Each component is calculated on the assessable value — typically the CIF (Cost, Insurance, and Freight) value of the goods. The rates vary significantly depending on the product's HS code classification, with essential goods like medicines and books attracting lower rates while luxury items like high-end cars face duties exceeding 300% of their value.

Bangladesh uses the Harmonized System (HS) codes to classify all imported goods. Each HS code maps to a specific set of duty rates. This calculator simplifies the process by grouping common products into categories with their approximate total duty rates, so you don't need to know the exact HS code to get a quick estimate.

How to Calculate Import Tax

To calculate your total import cost, start with the CIF value of your goods in BDT. The CIF value includes the product price, international shipping cost, and insurance. Each duty component is then calculated on the assessable value. While the simplified calculation treats each tax as a flat percentage of CIF value, the actual NBR method applies some duties cascadingly — for example, VAT is sometimes assessed on the value plus customs duty plus supplementary duty combined. This calculator uses simplified flat-rate totals for quick estimation.

For precise calculations, especially for commercial imports, always consult the Bangladesh Customs tariff schedule or hire a licensed customs clearing agent (C&F agent). The tariff schedule is updated through NBR Statutory Regulatory Orders (SROs) throughout the fiscal year, meaning rates can change with government notifications.

Tips to Reduce Customs Costs

There are several legal ways to minimize your customs duty burden in Bangladesh. First, check if your product qualifies for reduced rates under any Free Trade Agreement (FTA) or preferential trade agreement — Bangladesh has agreements with SAFTA, APTA, and other regional blocs. Second, ensure correct HS code classification; misclassification can lead to overpayment. Third, take advantage of bonded warehouse facilities if you're importing raw materials for manufacturing and re-export. Fourth, consider importing components separately if the assembled product attracts higher duty. Finally, keep detailed documentation of your CIF value to avoid customs valuation disputes that can lead to higher assessed duty.

For personal imports such as phones or laptops brought through airports, Bangladesh Customs allows a duty-free baggage allowance. Items beyond the allowance are subject to standard duty rates. Check the latest NBR circular for current duty-free limits before travelling.
