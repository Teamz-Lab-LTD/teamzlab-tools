How DOI Lookup Works

A Digital Object Identifier (DOI) is a unique alphanumeric string assigned to a published document — journal articles, conference papers, book chapters, datasets, and more. Every DOI resolves to a URL that points directly to the content, making it the most reliable way to locate scholarly work online. This DOI lookup tool queries the CrossRef metadata database, which holds records for over 150 million scholarly works, to retrieve structured information about any publication.

Simply paste your DOI (with or without the https://doi.org/ prefix), and the tool fetches the title, authors, journal name, publication date, volume, issue, page numbers, abstract, ISSN, document type, and citation count. Everything runs in your browser — no data is stored on our servers, and no account is required.

Understanding DOI Format and Structure

A DOI consists of two parts separated by a forward slash: a prefix (assigned to the publisher) and a suffix (assigned to the specific work). For example, in 10.1038/nature12373, the prefix 10.1038 identifies Nature Publishing Group, while nature12373 identifies the individual article. The 10. at the start is mandatory for all DOIs. Some DOIs contain additional slashes, parentheses, or special characters in the suffix — this tool handles all valid DOI formats.

If you have a full DOI URL like https://doi.org/10.1038/nature12373, you can paste it directly. The tool automatically extracts the DOI portion and queries CrossRef. DOIs are case-insensitive, permanent, and guaranteed to resolve to the correct resource even if the publisher changes their URL structure.

Citation Formats Explained

The citation generator supports three major academic formats. APA (American Psychological Association) is standard in psychology, education, and social sciences. MLA (Modern Language Association) is common in humanities, literature, and cultural studies. Chicago (notes-bibliography style) is used in history, arts, and many university presses. Each format has specific rules for author ordering, title italicization, date placement, and DOI inclusion — all handled automatically by this tool.
