About UCL All-Time Stats & Records

The UEFA Champions League is the most prestigious club football competition in the world, running since 1955 when it started as the European Cup. Over nearly seven decades, legendary clubs and players have etched their names into history with unforgettable performances, record-breaking goal tallies, and multiple title triumphs.

This reference page compiles the most important all-time records from the Champions League era, including data from the original European Cup format. Every stat is sortable and searchable, making it easy to settle debates and explore the history of European football's biggest competition.

Most UCL Titles

Real Madrid stand alone at the top with 15 European Cup/Champions League titles, more than double any other club. Their dominance spans from the competition's earliest days in the 1950s through to their modern-era triumphs. AC Milan sit second with seven titles, followed by Bayern Munich and Liverpool with six each. Barcelona, with five titles, round out the elite group of clubs with five or more European Cups.

All-Time Top Scorers

Cristiano Ronaldo holds the all-time scoring record with 140 Champions League goals, accumulated across spells at Manchester United, Real Madrid, and Juventus. Lionel Messi follows with 129 goals scored for Barcelona and Paris Saint-Germain. Robert Lewandowski is the only other player to surpass the 100-goal mark, with 101 goals for Borussia Dortmund, Bayern Munich, and Barcelona. Karim Benzema scored 90 goals primarily during his time at Real Madrid.

Most Appearances

Cristiano Ronaldo also leads the all-time appearances chart with 183 matches, reflecting his longevity at the top of European football. Iker Casillas made 177 appearances between Real Madrid and Porto, while Lionel Messi appeared in 163 matches. These numbers underscore the remarkable consistency required to compete at the highest level of European club football season after season.
