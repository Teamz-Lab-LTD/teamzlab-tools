How the UCL Watch Party Planner Works

Planning a Champions League watch party can be stressful when you are not sure how much food and drink to buy. This calculator takes the guesswork out by estimating quantities based on the number of guests, match duration, and your food and drink preferences. Whether it is a casual group stage evening or a tense final with extra time and penalties, this planner has you covered.

The tool uses standard catering estimates: approximately 2-3 slices of pizza per person per hour, 3-4 chicken wings per person, and 2-3 drinks per person over a standard match. For longer matches with extra time and penalties, quantities increase accordingly to keep your guests fueled through the drama.

Party Estimation Formulas

Pizzas = ceil(Guests × Slices Per Person / 8)

Wings = Guests × Wings Per Person

Drinks = Guests × Drinks Per Person

Total Cost = (Food Cost + Drink Cost) per person × Guests

Match Duration Guide

A standard UCL match lasts 90 minutes (two 45-minute halves plus 15-minute halftime). If the match goes to extra time, add 30 minutes for a total of 120 minutes. With a penalty shootout, plan for an additional 15 minutes beyond extra time, totaling approximately 135 minutes. Add pre-match buildup and post-match discussion and you could be hosting for 3+ hours.

Food Planning Tips

Pizza is the classic match day food: easy to share, minimal cleanup, and universally loved. For a group of 10, plan on 4-5 large pizzas. Wings are another popular choice, with 30-40 wings covering a group of 10 comfortably. For a mixed spread, combine smaller quantities of each. Always order slightly more than calculated because match day excitement drives appetite.

Budget Tips for Your UCL Party

Keep costs down by asking guests to bring a specific item. Set a per-person budget of 10-15 euros for a casual gathering or 20-30 euros for a premium experience. Consider bulk-buying drinks and snacks from wholesale stores. If watching at a pub instead of home, factor in drink minimums and potentially higher food costs but zero setup time.

Screen Setup Recommendations

For small groups of 4-6, a standard TV works perfectly. Groups of 8-15 benefit from a projector setup for that stadium-like atmosphere. Larger groups of 15+ should consider multiple screens or a venue booking. Whichever setup you choose, ensure everyone has a clear sightline and the audio is loud enough to hear commentary over the crowd noise in your living room.
