How the Mileage Calculator Works

This mileage calculator operates in two modes to cover every fuel-related question. In Trip Cost mode, you enter your planned distance, your vehicle's fuel efficiency, and the current fuel price. The calculator determines exactly how much fuel you need and what it will cost, including a per-mile or per-kilometer breakdown. In Fuel Efficiency mode, enter the distance you traveled and the amount of fuel you used, and the calculator computes your actual MPG, km/L, and L/100km so you can compare against manufacturer ratings.

Mileage Calculator Formulas

Fuel Needed = Distance ÷ Fuel Efficiency (MPG or km/L)

Total Cost = Fuel Needed × Fuel Price

Cost Per Mile = Total Cost ÷ Distance

MPG = Distance (miles) ÷ Fuel Used (gallons)

L/100km = (Fuel Used in L ÷ Distance in km) × 100

Tips to Improve Fuel Efficiency

Small changes in driving habits can meaningfully reduce fuel costs over time. Maintain steady speeds on the highway and use cruise control when possible — speed fluctuations waste fuel. Keep tires inflated to the recommended pressure, since underinflated tires increase rolling resistance by up to 3 percent. Remove roof racks and heavy cargo when not in use, as extra weight and aerodynamic drag force the engine to work harder. Avoid idling for more than 30 seconds; modern engines use less fuel restarting than idling. Regular oil changes with the manufacturer-recommended grade keep the engine running efficiently, and a clean air filter improves combustion and reduces consumption.

MPG vs L/100km — Understanding Fuel Economy

The United States measures fuel economy in miles per gallon (MPG), where a higher number is better. Most of Europe, Australia, and Asia use liters per 100 kilometers (L/100km), where a lower number is better. This inverse relationship can cause confusion when comparing vehicles across systems. For example, a car rated at 30 MPG consumes about 7.8 L/100km, while 40 MPG equals roughly 5.9 L/100km. Japan and parts of South America use km/L, which behaves like MPG — higher is more efficient. This calculator converts between all three systems automatically, so you always see your results in the format you prefer.

Mileage Calculator for Road Trips

Planning a road trip budget starts with accurate fuel cost estimation. Enter your total trip distance, your vehicle's real-world fuel efficiency (not the manufacturer sticker — check your trip computer), and the average fuel price along your route. The calculator returns total fuel needed, total cost, and cost per mile or kilometer. For multi-day trips, remember that highway driving is typically 10 to 15 percent more efficient than city driving, and fuel prices can vary significantly by region. Filling up before crossing into states or countries with higher fuel taxes can save meaningful amounts on longer journeys.
