How to Choose the Right Car Cover Size

Choosing the correct car cover size is essential for protecting your vehicle from weather, dust, bird droppings, tree sap, and UV damage. A cover that is too small will leave parts of the car exposed, while one that is too large can flap in the wind and scratch the paint. The ideal cover fits snugly around your car, reaching down to the bottom edge on all sides without dragging on the ground.

Car covers are typically sold in universal sizes from S (Small) to 3XL, based on the overall length of the vehicle. Small covers fit hatchbacks and city cars under 4,200 mm (165 inches), while 3XL covers are designed for full-size pickup trucks and extra-large SUVs over 5,400 mm (213 inches). The most popular sizes are M and L, which fit the majority of sedans and compact SUVs on the road today. Our finder uses precise manufacturer dimensions to match your specific make and model to the right universal size.

For the best fit, always measure your car from the front bumper to the rear bumper and compare it to the size chart. If your car falls near the boundary between two sizes, choose the larger size — a slightly loose cover is far better than one that does not fully protect the vehicle. Consider your car's height and width as well: tall SUVs and trucks with roof racks may need a size up from what the length alone suggests.

Step-by-Step Car Cover Wrapping Guide

Putting on a car cover properly takes just a few minutes and extends the life of both the cover and your car's finish. Follow these steps for a secure, scratch-free fit every time:

  1. Park on a flat, clean surface. Remove loose debris from the car's surface — leaves, dust, and grit trapped under the cover will scratch the paint.
  2. Unfold the cover on the roof, centered. Place the folded cover on top of the roof, roughly centered between front and rear. Let it drape naturally.
  3. Pull the front flap down over the hood. Walk to the front and pull the cover forward until it aligns with the front bumper. Tuck it snugly around the grille and headlights.
  4. Pull the rear flap down over the trunk. Walk to the rear and pull the cover back until it reaches the rear bumper. Ensure taillights and rear fenders are covered.
  5. Tuck the side panels down and secure mirror pockets. If your cover has mirror pockets, align them over the side mirrors. Smooth the side panels down so the cover sits flush against the doors.
  6. Secure with elastic hem, buckle straps, or tie-downs. Most quality covers have an elastic hem that grips the bottom edge. Use any included buckle straps or tie-down grommets underneath the car for extra security.
  7. For wind protection, use a cable lock through grommets. In windy areas, thread a cable lock through the front and rear grommets and secure it under the car to prevent the cover from blowing off.

Tips: Always cover a dry, clean car to prevent moisture trapping. Remove the cover in reverse order (sides first, then rear, then front). Shake off debris before folding and store the cover in a breathable bag to prevent mildew.

Car Cover Materials: Indoor vs Outdoor

The material of your car cover matters as much as the size. Indoor covers are typically made from soft, breathable fabrics like cotton, flannel, or satin-stretch materials. They protect against dust, accidental scratches, and minor bumps in the garage but are not waterproof. For indoor-only use, choose a soft cover that will not mar the finish.

Outdoor covers need to handle rain, snow, UV rays, bird droppings, and wind. Look for multi-layer covers with a waterproof outer shell and a soft inner lining. Polypropylene and polyester blends with UV inhibitors are the most popular choices. Fully waterproof covers are excellent for rain protection but must have ventilation to prevent moisture buildup underneath. Breathable waterproof covers (like those using a membrane similar to Gore-Tex) offer the best of both worlds — they repel rain while allowing trapped moisture to evaporate. For hot climates, reflective silver covers reduce heat absorption and protect the interior from UV damage.

Cover Size Chart

  • S (Small): Under 4,200 mm (165 in) — Hatchbacks, city cars
  • M (Medium): 4,200 – 4,600 mm (165 – 181 in) — Compact sedans, small SUVs
  • L (Large): 4,600 – 4,900 mm (181 – 193 in) — Mid-size sedans, SUVs
  • XL (Extra Large): 4,900 – 5,200 mm (193 – 205 in) — Full-size sedans, large SUVs
  • XXL: 5,200 – 5,400 mm (205 – 213 in) — Full-size SUVs, mid-size trucks
  • 3XL: Over 5,400 mm (213 in) — Full-size trucks, XL SUVs
