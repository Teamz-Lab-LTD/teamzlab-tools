How the Remote Work Cost Calculator Works

This calculator compares the true annual cost of your current work arrangement — fully remote, hybrid, or full office — by adding up every major expense category. Commuting is usually the biggest variable: the calculator converts your one-way distance, commute method, and fuel efficiency (or transit pass cost) into an annual figure based on how many office days your arrangement requires. A fully remote worker commutes zero days; a full office worker commutes roughly 260 days per year (52 weeks × 5 days), adjusted for public holidays.

Lunch costs are calculated by comparing what you spend on office lunches (takeaway, restaurant, or cafe) versus cooking or preparing food at home. Over a full year, even a small daily difference of $5 adds up to over $1,000. Home electricity is costed at your stated daily rate multiplied by the number of working-from-home days per year — capturing the extra heating, cooling, and screen time that comes with being home all day.

Remote Work Savings by Work Arrangement

Research consistently shows that fully remote workers save the most: between $2,000 and $7,000 per year compared to full office workers, depending on city and commute distance. Hybrid workers (2–3 days in the office) typically save $1,000–$4,000 annually while avoiding the social isolation of fully remote work. The financial benefit of going remote is largest for workers in expensive cities like Sydney, London, or Singapore where office lunches can easily cost $15–$25 per day and public transport passes are $150–$300/month.

Wardrobe costs are often underestimated. Office workers typically spend $500–$1,500 per year on work-specific clothing, dry cleaning, and accessories. Remote workers who rarely go to the office can cut this to near zero — a saving that compounds over multiple years.

Time Savings: The Hidden Value

Beyond money, commuting costs time. A 30-minute one-way commute means 1 hour per day, which adds up to 260 hours per year for a full office worker — equivalent to more than 6 full 40-hour work weeks. This calculator shows your annual time saved and converts it to an effective hourly savings rate, helping you understand the full value of each work arrangement. Many workers find that the time savings of remote work are worth more to them than the financial savings.

Tax Deductions for Remote Workers

In many countries, remote workers can claim home office deductions to offset some of their electricity and workspace costs. The Australian Tax Office (ATO) offers a fixed rate of $0.70 per hour worked from home. The UK's HMRC allows a weekly flat rate. The US IRS offers a simplified method of $5 per square foot (up to 300 sq ft) for a dedicated home office. Always check current rules with a qualified tax professional in your country to ensure you claim everything you are entitled to.
