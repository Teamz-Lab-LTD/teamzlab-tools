How to Create a Freelance Proposal

Fill in your details, client information, project scope, deliverables, timeline, and pricing. Click "Generate Proposal" to preview a professional document. Download as PDF to send to your client. A strong proposal clearly communicates what you will deliver, when, and for how much — setting expectations and protecting both parties.

What Makes a Winning Freelance Proposal

The best proposals focus on the client's problem, not your resume. Start with a project summary that shows you understand their goals. List specific deliverables with clear descriptions. Provide a realistic timeline with milestones. Present pricing confidently — either fixed price for defined scope or hourly for flexible engagements. Include payment terms and a validity date to create urgency.

Freelance Proposal vs Contract

A proposal outlines what you will do, when, and for how much — it is a sales document. A contract is a legally binding agreement with detailed terms, conditions, and legal protections. Use this proposal generator to win the project, then formalize with a contract. Many freelancers include basic terms in the proposal (payment schedule, revisions) that carry into the contract.

Pricing Tips for Freelancers

Fixed price works best for well-defined projects with clear deliverables. Hourly rate is better for ongoing work or projects with uncertain scope. Monthly retainer suits long-term relationships with recurring work. Always include your payment schedule — 50% upfront is standard for new clients. Add a late payment clause to protect your cash flow.
