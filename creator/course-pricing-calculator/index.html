How Online Course Pricing Works

Pricing an online course is one of the most consequential decisions a course creator makes, yet most creators either underprice their courses out of insecurity or choose a price based on gut feeling rather than data. The optimal course price should reflect three fundamental factors: the investment required to create the course, the number of students you realistically expect to enroll, and the perceived value of the learning experience you deliver. When these three factors are properly balanced, you arrive at a price point that covers your costs, generates a healthy profit, and feels fair to students who receive genuine transformation through your course content.

This calculator approaches pricing from a cost-recovery foundation and then applies value multipliers based on the depth and features of your course offering. The minimum viable price is calculated by dividing your total creation investment by the net revenue per student after platform fees. The value multiplier then adjusts this minimum upward based on factors that increase the perceived and actual value of your course, such as longer content duration, community access, and certificate completion. The result is a suggested price range that ensures profitability while reflecting the premium value of a well-structured course. The calculator also projects annual revenue and net income after platform fees, giving you a clear picture of the financial outcome at your suggested price point.

Course Pricing Formulas

Creation Investment = Hours to Create × Hourly Value

Minimum Price = Creation Investment ÷ (Target Students × (1 - Platform Fee % ÷ 100))

Value Multiplier = Base (from content hours) × Community Bonus × Certificate Bonus

Suggested Price = Minimum Price × Value Multiplier

Annual Revenue = Suggested Price × Target Students

Net After Fees = Annual Revenue × (1 - Platform Fee % ÷ 100)

Where:

  • Hours to Create = Total hours invested in planning, recording, editing, and building the course
  • Hourly Value = The dollar value assigned to each hour of your creation time
  • Target Students = Expected number of enrolled students in the first year
  • Platform Fee % = Percentage taken by the course platform (e.g., Udemy, Teachable, Kajabi)
  • Value Multiplier = Adjustment based on content hours, community support, and certification

Understanding the Value Multiplier

Content Hours and Depth

The amount of course content directly influences its perceived value and the appropriate price point. A one-hour introductory course on a narrow topic warrants a different price than a comprehensive 20-hour masterclass. Research across major course platforms shows that courses with more content hours command significantly higher prices, but the relationship is not linear. Courses under two hours of content are typically priced between $19 and $49. Courses with two to five hours of content generally price between $49 and $149. Courses in the five to ten hour range command $99 to $299, and comprehensive courses exceeding ten hours of content can justify prices of $199 to $999 or more. The value multiplier in this calculator uses a base factor derived from content hours, starting at 1.5x for shorter courses and scaling up to 3.0x or more for extensive programs, reflecting the premium value that in-depth education delivers to students.

Community and Support Features

Including community access or ongoing support with your course significantly increases its value proposition and justifies a higher price. Community features can include a private discussion forum, a Slack or Discord group, live Q&A sessions, office hours, or peer accountability groups. When students have access to a community, they receive value beyond the recorded content because they can ask questions, share progress, network with peers, and receive feedback from the instructor. This ongoing interaction increases course completion rates, improves learning outcomes, and creates a sticky experience that leads to higher satisfaction and more referrals. The community bonus multiplier of 1.3x reflects the approximately 30% increase in perceived value that community access adds to a course offering, based on pricing data from platforms like Teachable, Kajabi, and Podia.

Certificate of Completion

Offering a certificate of completion adds both practical and psychological value to your course. From a practical standpoint, students can display certificates on LinkedIn profiles, include them in resumes, and present them to employers as evidence of professional development. From a psychological standpoint, a certificate provides a tangible sense of accomplishment that motivates students to complete the course and increases their perception of its legitimacy. The certificate bonus multiplier of 1.2x reflects the additional value that certification adds to the student experience. Certificates are especially valuable in professional development, technical skills, and compliance-related courses where employers recognize or require continuing education credentials.

Example Calculations

Example 1: Basic Course, No Community or Certificate

A creator spends 80 hours building a course, values time at $75/hr, expects 100 students, 30% platform fee, 5 hours of content.

  • Creation Investment = 80 × $75 = $6,000
  • Minimum Price = $6,000 ÷ (100 × 0.70) = $85.71
  • Value Multiplier = 2.0 (5 hours content) × 1.0 × 1.0 = 2.0
  • Suggested Price = $85.71 × 2.0 = $171.43
  • Annual Revenue = $171.43 × 100 = $17,142.86
  • Net After Fees = $17,142.86 × 0.70 = $12,000.00

Example 2: Premium Course with Community and Certificate

A creator spends 200 hours, values time at $100/hr, expects 150 students, 15% platform fee, 12 hours of content, community and certificate included.

  • Creation Investment = 200 × $100 = $20,000
  • Minimum Price = $20,000 ÷ (150 × 0.85) = $156.86
  • Value Multiplier = 3.0 (12 hours content) × 1.3 (community) × 1.2 (certificate) = 4.68
  • Suggested Price = $156.86 × 4.68 = $734.11
  • Annual Revenue = $734.11 × 150 = $110,116.47
  • Net After Fees = $110,116.47 × 0.85 = $93,599.00

Choosing the Right Platform Fee Structure

Different course platforms have vastly different fee structures, and the platform you choose directly impacts your pricing strategy. Udemy takes up to 63% of revenue when they drive the sale through their marketplace, making it challenging for premium pricing but excellent for volume. Teachable charges a flat monthly subscription plus a 5% transaction fee on lower-tier plans, which is more favorable for higher-priced courses. Kajabi charges a monthly subscription with no transaction fees, making it ideal for premium courses priced above $200. Thinkific offers a free tier with transaction fees and paid tiers without them. When entering your platform fee percentage in this calculator, use the effective percentage that reflects both transaction fees and any revenue-sharing arrangements. Understanding your true platform costs ensures that your minimum price calculation accurately reflects what you need to charge to recover your creation investment.

When to Price Higher vs. Lower

The decision to price your course higher or lower than the suggested price depends on your market positioning and business model. Higher pricing works best when your course targets professionals who can expense the purchase, when you offer a genuine transformation or career advancement outcome, when your personal brand carries significant authority in the subject area, and when you include substantial support and community features. Lower pricing works better when you are building your initial student base and need reviews and testimonials, when your course covers a topic with heavy free competition on YouTube or blogs, when you plan to upsell students into higher-tier offerings, or when your target market is price-sensitive students or hobbyists. Many successful course creators start with a lower launch price to generate enrollment momentum and social proof, then gradually increase the price as the course accumulates positive reviews and demonstrated results.
