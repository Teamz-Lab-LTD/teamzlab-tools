What is vSME and Who Needs It

The Voluntary SME standard (vSME) is a sustainability reporting framework published by EFRAG in December 2024 for small and medium enterprises that fall below the CSRD mandatory threshold. It gives SMEs a single, standardised format to answer the growing flood of ESG data requests from larger buyers, banks, and insurers — replacing dozens of one-off questionnaires with one EFRAG-aligned report. If your customers, lenders, or procurement partners are CSRD-reportable, they need structured data from their supply chain, and vSME is the agreed standard.

Basic vs Comprehensive Module

The Basic Module covers the essentials almost every buyer asks for: company profile, energy use, greenhouse gas estimates, water, waste, workforce metrics, and governance. It is designed to take an SME a few hours, not weeks, and it is the right starting point for micro and small enterprises. The Comprehensive Module adds deeper disclosures — Scope 1/2/3 emissions breakdown, climate transition narrative, a preliminary double-materiality check, and targets. Medium SMEs (50–249 employees) or any SME supplying a listed company should use Comprehensive. This generator produces both, side by side, so you can share the level of detail your counterparty actually needs.

Beyond vSME: When You Need Full CSRD

vSME is voluntary. Once an SME crosses 250 employees, €50M turnover, or €25M balance sheet (two of three, two years running), it becomes subject to the full CSRD and the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS). At that point Scope 3 emissions, formal double-materiality assessment, climate transition plans, and third-party assurance are mandatory — vSME is not a substitute.
