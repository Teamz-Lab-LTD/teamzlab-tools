How to Create a SWOT Analysis

Enter your project or company name, then fill in each quadrant. Strengths are internal advantages — what you do well. Weaknesses are internal limitations — what needs improvement. Opportunities are external factors you can leverage — market trends, partnerships. Threats are external risks — competition, regulations. Enter one item per line and click "Generate" to create a clean visual diagram you can download.

When to Use SWOT Analysis

SWOT analysis is essential for strategic planning, business plans, product launches, competitive analysis, and career development. Use it during quarterly planning sessions, before entering new markets, when evaluating mergers or partnerships, or when developing marketing strategies. It provides a structured framework that ensures you consider both internal capabilities and external environment.

SWOT Analysis for Startups and Small Businesses

For startups, SWOT is especially valuable because it forces honest assessment of your position. List your unique technical advantage as a strength, your limited funding as a weakness, a growing TAM as an opportunity, and established competitors as a threat. Investors expect to see SWOT analysis in pitch decks — this tool generates presentation-ready diagrams instantly.

Tips for Effective SWOT Analysis

Be specific — "good team" is vague, "3 engineers with 10+ years ML experience" is actionable. Prioritize items — list the most impactful factors first. Be honest about weaknesses — the value of SWOT comes from acknowledging gaps. Review quarterly — your SWOT changes as the market evolves. Use the output as input for your strategic plan, not as the plan itself.
