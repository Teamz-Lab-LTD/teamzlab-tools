How the Email Marketing Metrics Calculator Works

The email marketing metrics calculator is a free browser-based tool that computes 8 essential email campaign KPIs from your raw campaign data. Enter the number of emails sent, delivered, opened, clicked, bounced, and unsubscribed along with conversions and revenue. The calculator applies standard email marketing formulas used by platforms like Mailchimp, Klaviyo, and HubSpot. Open rate equals opens divided by delivered emails times 100. Click-through rate (CTR) equals clicks divided by delivered times 100. Click-to-open rate (CTOR) equals clicks divided by opens times 100. Each metric is compared against industry benchmarks based on 2024 Mailchimp and Campaign Monitor data covering over 1 billion emails sent. All calculations run locally in your browser with zero data transmitted to any server.

Email Marketing Benchmarks and Grading

Industry average open rate is approximately 21.33 percent according to Mailchimp's 2024 benchmark report across all industries. Average CTR sits at 2.62 percent while a healthy CTOR ranges from 10 to 15 percent. Bounce rates should stay below 2 percent to maintain sender reputation with ISPs. Unsubscribe rates above 0.5 percent per campaign signal content or frequency problems. The calculator grades each metric as above average, at average, or below average so you can quickly identify which areas of your email program need attention. Revenue per email and conversion rate depend heavily on your industry and offer but tracking them campaign over campaign reveals trends in list quality and engagement.

How to Improve Your Email Marketing KPIs

To improve open rates focus on subject line testing, send time optimization, and list hygiene by removing inactive subscribers every 90 days. Improve CTR by using clear call-to-action buttons, personalized content blocks, and mobile-responsive templates since over 60 percent of emails are opened on mobile devices according to Litmus 2024 data. Reduce bounce rates by implementing double opt-in and regularly cleaning invalid addresses. Monitor CTOR separately from CTR because CTOR measures content relevance among engaged readers while CTR includes everyone. A high open rate with low CTOR means your subject lines work but your content does not match expectations. Track list growth rate monthly to ensure acquisition outpaces natural attrition of 2 to 3 percent per month.
