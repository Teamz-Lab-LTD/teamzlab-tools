One-Page Business Plan for Startups

A one-page business plan is the modern alternative to 30-page traditional plans. Investors, accelerators, and partners prefer concise documents that communicate the key elements quickly. This format is used by Y Combinator, Techstars, and top VCs worldwide. It forces clarity — if you cannot explain your business on one page, the idea needs more refinement.

What Investors Look for in a Business Plan

Investors evaluate: Problem-Solution Fit (is the problem real and the solution compelling?), Market Size (is the TAM large enough?), Competitive Advantage (what is your moat?), Business Model (how do you make money?), Team (can this team execute?), and Financial Projections (are the numbers realistic?). This generator guides you through each element.

Business Plan vs Lean Canvas vs Pitch Deck

A business plan is a structured overview of your entire business. A lean canvas is a single-page visual framework (9 blocks). A pitch deck is a slide presentation (10-15 slides) for investor meetings. This tool generates a business plan — the foundation document from which you can create lean canvases and pitch decks.
