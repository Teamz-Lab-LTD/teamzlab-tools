How Roman Numeral Conversion Works

Roman numerals use seven symbols: I (1), V (5), X (10), L (50), C (100), D (500), and M (1000). Numbers are formed by combining these symbols and adding their values. When a smaller numeral appears before a larger one, it is subtracted (e.g., IV = 4, IX = 9).

Roman Numeral Rules

Addition: VI = 5 + 1 = 6, XII = 10 + 1 + 1 = 12

Subtraction: IV = 5 − 1 = 4, XC = 100 − 10 = 90

Maximum: The largest standard Roman numeral is MMMCMXCIX = 3999

Common Uses of Roman Numerals

Roman numerals are used in clock faces, book chapters, movie sequels (Star Wars Episode IV), Super Bowl numbering (Super Bowl LVIII), building cornerstones, outlines, and copyright dates on films and TV shows. Understanding Roman numerals helps you read dates on historic buildings and monuments.
