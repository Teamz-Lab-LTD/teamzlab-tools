Comparing Cheese Nutrition

Not all cheeses are created equal nutritionally. A serving of parmesan packs far more protein per calorie than cream cheese, while mozzarella is significantly lower in calories than cheddar. Use the compare tab to see two cheeses side by side on every nutrient, with the better value highlighted. Cheese in a Balanced Diet

Cheese is an excellent source of calcium, protein, and essential vitamins like B12 and vitamin A. However, it is also high in saturated fat and sodium, so portion control matters. A typical serving is about one ounce or 28 grams, roughly the size of a pair of dice. Hard cheeses like parmesan and Swiss tend to be higher in protein and calcium per serving, while soft cheeses like brie and cream cheese are higher in fat. Cottage cheese and ricotta are popular choices for high-protein, lower-calorie diets.

Healthiest Cheese Options by Goal

For weight loss, choose lower-calorie options like cottage cheese, mozzarella, or feta. For muscle building, pick high-protein cheeses like parmesan, Swiss, or gruyere. For bone health, focus on calcium-rich varieties like parmesan, gruyere, and cheddar. For lower sodium diets, Swiss, ricotta, and cream cheese are your best bets.
