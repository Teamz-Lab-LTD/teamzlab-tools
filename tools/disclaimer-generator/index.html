What Is a Disclaimer?

A disclaimer is a legal statement that limits the liability of your website or business. It informs users that the information on your site is for general purposes only and should not be relied upon as professional advice. Most websites, blogs, and apps need some form of disclaimer to protect against potential legal claims.

Common Disclaimer Types

General: Limits liability for information accuracy

Affiliate: Discloses affiliate link relationships (FTC required)

Medical: States content is not medical advice

Financial: States content is not financial advice

Legal: States content is not legal advice

Why Every Website Needs a Disclaimer

Disclaimers help protect you from liability if someone relies on your content and experiences negative results. For affiliate marketers, the FTC requires disclosure of affiliate relationships. Health and fitness blogs should clarify that content is not a substitute for professional medical advice. Even personal blogs benefit from a general disclaimer.

Is a Generated Disclaimer Legally Valid?

Generated disclaimers provide a solid starting point and cover common liability areas. However, for complex business situations, consult with a lawyer to ensure your disclaimer adequately protects your specific needs.
