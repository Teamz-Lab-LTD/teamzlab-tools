How Voice-to-Text Transcription Works in Your Browser

This tool uses Whisper, OpenAI's state-of-the-art speech recognition model, running locally in your browser through Transformers.js. Whisper was trained on 680,000 hours of multilingual audio and delivers remarkable accuracy for English transcription.

When you upload an audio file or record from your microphone, the audio is decoded locally into a waveform, then processed by the Whisper neural network — all without any data leaving your device. The model is downloaded once (~75MB) and cached for future offline use.

How It Works

  • Audio input: Upload a file (MP3, WAV, M4A, OGG, MP4, WebM) or record directly from your microphone.
  • Audio decoding: The file is decoded into raw waveform data using the Web Audio API — all locally.
  • AI transcription: Whisper processes the audio and generates text with timestamps.
  • Output: The transcribed text appears on screen. Copy or download as .txt.

Use Cases for Speech-to-Text

Meeting Transcription

Record your meetings and transcribe them instantly without relying on cloud-based services. Keep sensitive business discussions private while getting accurate written records.

Lecture Notes and Study

Upload lecture recordings and get text transcripts you can search, highlight, and study. Ideal for students who want to focus on listening rather than note-taking during class.

Create transcripts for podcasts, YouTube videos, or interviews. Use the text for blog posts, show notes, subtitles, or SEO content — all without paying for transcription services.

Make audio content accessible to hearing-impaired users by generating text versions. This tool helps content creators meet accessibility standards without costly services.

Privacy-First Transcription

Unlike services like Otter.ai or Google Transcribe that upload your audio to cloud servers, this tool processes everything on your device. Your audio files, voice recordings, and transcripts never leave your browser. This is critical for confidential meetings, legal recordings, medical dictation, or any sensitive audio content.
