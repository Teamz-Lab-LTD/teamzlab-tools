How to Create a Photo Collage

Making a photo collage is simple with our free online tool. Start by uploading 2 to 9 photos using the drag-and-drop area or the file browser. Once your photos are loaded, select a layout template that fits your vision. The tool offers 12 carefully designed templates including grids, asymmetric layouts, and creative arrangements. Adjust the gap spacing, border radius, and background color to match your style. The live preview updates instantly so you can experiment freely before downloading your finished collage in high-definition PNG format.

Collage Templates and Layout Options

Choose from a variety of professionally designed layout templates to showcase your photos. For two photos, pick side-by-side or top-and-bottom arrangements. Three-photo layouts include one large feature image with two smaller companions, three equal columns, or a top strip with two below. Four-photo options range from the classic 2x2 grid to one dominant image with three supporting shots. For larger collections, use the 2x3, 3x2, or 3x3 grids to display six or nine photos in a clean, organized format. Every template automatically crops your photos to fill each cell while maintaining the correct aspect ratio, so you always get a polished result regardless of your original photo dimensions.

Tips for Professional-Looking Collages

To create collages that look polished and intentional, keep a few design principles in mind. Use photos with similar lighting and color tones for a cohesive appearance. The gap slider lets you add breathing room between images — even 4 to 8 pixels of spacing can make a dramatic difference in readability. Adding a subtle border radius softens hard edges and gives your collage a modern feel. When choosing a background color, white works for clean and minimal looks, while dark backgrounds make photos pop with dramatic contrast. For social media, the square 1080x1080 format is optimized for Instagram feed posts, and the 1080x1350 portrait format takes up more screen space in the feed for better engagement. Always use the highest-resolution source photos available for the sharpest collage output.
