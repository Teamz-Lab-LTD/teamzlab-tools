How Swin2SR AI Face Restoration Works

This AI Face Restorer uses Swin2SR — a Swin Transformer V2-based image restoration model from Microsoft Research — running via Transformers.js in your browser. Unlike simple Canvas sharpening, Swin2SR understands image structure and reconstructs fine detail genuinely lost to age and scanning: facial features, hair strands, skin texture edges. The model is downloaded once (~50MB) and cached for offline use. After the AI pass, a portrait-specific post-processing pipeline applies gentle noise reduction and targeted sharpening calibrated for skin tones. Last updated: March 2026.

The restoration pipeline: your photo is resized to max 512px before the AI (keeping processing fast), fed through Swin2SR for super-resolution reconstruction, then post-processed with contrast and sharpening at the mode level you select (Gentle / Standard / Strong). If the AI model is unavailable the tool falls back to the full Canvas pipeline automatically — no broken experience.

Portrait Enhancement Modes

  • Gentle mode: Minimal post-processing on top of AI (contrast 8, light noise reduction, sharpAmount 0.15). Best for photos that are already reasonably clear — let the AI do the heavy lifting without over-processing.
  • Standard mode: Balanced post-processing (contrast 15, moderate noise reduction, sharpAmount 0.3). Best for most old portrait photos — restores contrast, smooths grain, sharpens key features.
  • Strong mode: Maximum post-processing on top of AI (contrast 25, strong noise reduction, sharpAmount 0.5). Best for heavily deteriorated photos — use with care on recent or already-clear photos.

Face Restoration vs General Photo Enhancement

General photo enhancement boosts contrast, saturation, and sharpness uniformly. For landscapes and products that works well. For portraits, uniform sharpening creates "halo" artefacts around hair and clothing. The AI Face Restorer uses portrait-calibrated post-processing: lower contrast amounts that protect skin gradients, radius-limited sharpening that targets small facial detail (faces typically 200–600px wide) while leaving large background elements unchanged. The result is sharper eyes and lips without the "over-sharpened" ring artefact.
