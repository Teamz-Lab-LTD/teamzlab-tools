How AI Face Restoration Works

This AI Face Restorer applies a portrait-optimised multi-step pipeline tuned specifically for human faces. Generic photo enhancement tools use the same parameters for all photo types. This tool uses parameters calibrated for portrait photography: gentler contrast curves that protect skin tone gradients, an unsharp mask radius and amount chosen to sharpen eyes, lips, and hair without creating the "over-sharpened" artefact ring that appears around edges when sharpening is too aggressive. The pipeline runs in three passes: first a tonal restoration that corrects exposure and colour fading; then a portrait-specific noise reduction that smooths skin grain without flattening skin texture; then an edge-targeted sharpening that enhances high-contrast portrait features — eyes, eyebrows, lips, and hair — which are typically the most impactful elements in a portrait.

Portrait Enhancement Best Practices

  • Gentle mode: Best for photos where faces are mostly clear — adds subtle definition without over-processing.
  • Standard mode: Best for most old portrait photos — restores contrast, removes grain, sharpens key features.
  • Strong mode: Best for heavily deteriorated photos — maximum tonal correction and sharpening, use with care on recent photos.

Face Restoration vs General Photo Enhancement

General photo enhancement boosts contrast, saturation, and sharpness uniformly across the image. This works well for landscapes and product photos. For portraits, uniform sharpening creates "halo" artefacts around hair and clothing that look unnatural. Portrait-specific enhancement uses lower contrast amounts and radius-limited sharpening that targets small-scale detail (faces typically 200–600px wide) while leaving large background elements unchanged.
