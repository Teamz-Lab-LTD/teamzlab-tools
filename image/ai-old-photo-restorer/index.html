How AI Photo Restoration Works

This AI Old Photo Restorer applies a multi-pass enhancement pipeline directly in your browser using the Canvas API. Old photos typically suffer from four problems: tonal compression (fading), colour cast (yellow-brown shift), physical damage (scratches, dust), and loss of fine detail (blur from age and scanning). The restoration pipeline addresses each in sequence.

First, auto-levels stretches the histogram — the darkest pixels become true black and brightest become true white, recovering full tonal range lost to fading. Next, a contrast enhancement pass boosts local contrast so faces and textures become more defined. A dust and scratch reduction step uses a spatial filter to smooth high-frequency anomalies consistent with physical damage. Finally, an adaptive unsharp mask recovers edge sharpness. What Our AI Fixes

  • Fading: Tonal range compression from decades of light exposure — auto-levels restores full contrast.
  • Yellowing: Colour cast from paper aging — colour correction neutralises the yellow-brown shift.
  • Scratches and dust: Physical surface damage — spatial filtering smooths anomalous bright/dark lines.
  • Soft detail: Blurring from old lenses or scanning — unsharp mask recovers edge definition.
