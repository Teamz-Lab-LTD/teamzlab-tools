# AlwaysReady Care — UK Social Care Compliance Market Research (2026)

## Executive Summary

AlwaysReady Care is entering a real gap. The UK care-tech market is crowded with full care-management systems that bundle care planning, rostering, eMAR, family portals, payroll, and HR. But CQC's current assessment approach is increasingly about structured, retrievable, multi-source evidence against the 5 key questions, 34 quality statements, and 6 evidence categories. That makes a credible wedge for a compliance-only evidence layer that sits on top of existing systems rather than replacing them.

For England, the practical CQC-facing market is about **29,719 service locations**: 10,301 care homes without nursing, 4,186 care homes with nursing, and 15,232 non-residential locations (domiciliary care, supported living, extra care housing).

A realistic England-first subscription TAM is roughly **£28m–£64m ARR**, depending on pricing at £79–£179 per service/month. At mid-point £129/service/month, modeled TAM is about **£46.0m ARR**.

## 2. CQC Assessment Framework

### 5 Key Questions
1. Safe
2. Effective
3. Caring
4. Responsive
5. Well-led

### 34 Quality Statements across 6 Evidence Categories
1. People's experience
2. Feedback from staff and leaders
3. Feedback from partners
4. Observation
5. Processes
6. Outcomes

### What Inspectors Look For
- Care plans, needs assessments, reviews, risk assessments, MCA/DoLS documentation
- Medication evidence (MAR/eMAR, PRN protocols, audit trails)
- Safeguarding and incident evidence (allegation logs, referrals, outcomes, learning)
- Complaints, feedback, communication, responsive-care evidence
- Staffing, supervision, wellbeing, training, competency evidence
- Governance evidence (audits, action plans, quality meetings, improvement plans)
- Direct observation of staff practice, interactions, environment
- Record quality (accurate, complete, up to date, securely handled)

### Common Reasons for "Requires Improvement" or "Inadequate"
1. Weak governance and poor record control (Reg 17)
2. Unsafe care and treatment (Reg 12)
3. Staffing problems (Reg 18)
4. Consent/MCA/DoLS gaps (Reg 11)
5. Safeguarding/incident/complaint handling gaps
6. Person-centred evidence too thin

## 5. Pain Points & Buying Triggers

### Top 5 Daily Frustrations
1. Evidence scattered across paper, WhatsApp, Excel, email
2. Audits don't close the loop (no proof of action taken)
3. Records are retrospective and patchy (staffing pressure)
4. Inspection readiness is episodic, not continuous
5. Governance visibility weak across multiple homes

### Buying Triggers
- "Requires Improvement" or "Inadequate" CQC rating
- New registered manager
- Upcoming/feared inspection
- Serious incident or medication error
- Shift from paper to digital
- Multi-site growth creating inconsistency

### Sales Cycle
- Independent homes: 30–90 days
- Small groups: 2–4 months
- Large chains: 4–9 months

### Decision Makers
- Single home: registered manager + owner
- Small group: registered manager + ops lead + owner
- Large chain: nominated individual + quality director + procurement

- Retention: Adult care records 8 years, serious incidents 20 years, non-serious 10 years
