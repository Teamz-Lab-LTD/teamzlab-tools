How Much Does a Fence Cost?

Fence costs range from $5-$50+ per linear foot depending on material and height. The average homeowner spends $2,000-$8,000 on a new fence for a typical backyard (150-200 linear feet). Chain-link is the most affordable at $5-$15/ft. Wood privacy fencing costs $12-$27/ft. Vinyl fencing costs $20-$40/ft. Aluminum costs $25-$40/ft. Composite and wrought iron are premium options at $30-$60+/ft. Labor typically adds $5-$15 per linear foot to material costs.

Fence Material Comparison

Chain-link is cheapest and requires no maintenance but offers minimal privacy. Wood is the most popular choice, offering privacy and aesthetics at moderate cost — cedar and redwood resist rot naturally. Vinyl fencing requires zero maintenance and lasts 20-30 years but costs 2-3 times more than wood upfront. Aluminum provides an ornamental look without the rust issues of wrought iron. Composite fencing combines recycled wood and plastic for a wood look with vinyl durability but at premium prices. Consider lifespan, maintenance costs, and aesthetics when choosing.

Factors Affecting Fence Cost

Length is the primary cost factor — measure your property lines accurately. Height increases costs roughly 30-50% from 4ft to 6ft and another 30% to 8ft. Gates add $200-$600 each depending on material and width. Terrain affects labor costs — rocky soil, slopes, and tree roots increase installation time 20-40%. Permit costs range from $20-$200 in most areas. Old fence removal adds $3-$5 per linear foot. Post spacing (6ft vs 8ft apart) affects material costs. Always get property surveys and check HOA restrictions before installing.
