How to Plan a Wedding Budget

Start by determining your total budget from savings, family contributions, and any other sources. The average wedding cost varies significantly by region, but typical breakdowns allocate about 40-50% to venue and catering, 10-15% to photography and videography, 8-10% to attire, 8-10% to flowers and decorations, and the rest to music, invitations, transportation, and miscellaneous items. This calculator uses industry-standard percentages to give you a realistic starting point.

Wedding Budget by Guest Count

Guest count is the single biggest driver of wedding costs. Each additional guest increases catering, venue space, invitations, favors, and seating costs. A wedding with 50 guests costs significantly less than one with 200. When budgeting, estimate your per-guest cost by dividing total catering and venue costs by guest count. If your per-guest cost exceeds your budget comfort zone, consider reducing the guest list or finding more affordable venue and catering options.

Tips to Save on Your Wedding

Choose an off-peak season or weekday for lower venue rates. Consider all-inclusive venue packages that bundle catering, decorations, and coordination. DIY elements like centerpieces, invitations, and favors can save thousands. Book vendors early to lock in current prices. Prioritize spending on what matters most to you and cut costs on things guests rarely notice. Set aside 5-10% of your budget as a contingency fund for unexpected expenses that inevitably arise during wedding planning.
