What Is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation using height and weight to estimate body fat. It is widely used as a screening tool but is not a diagnostic measure. BMI does not distinguish between muscle and fat, so athletes may have a high BMI without excess fat.

BMI Formulas

BMI (metric) = Weight (kg) / Height (m)²

BMI (imperial) = (Weight (lbs) × 703) / Height (inches)²

Categories: Underweight <18.5 | Normal 18.5-24.9 | Overweight 25-29.9 | Obese 30+

BMI Limitations

BMI is a screening tool, not a diagnosis. It does not account for muscle mass, bone density, age, sex, or fat distribution. Consult a healthcare provider for a comprehensive health assessment.
