How CAC Is Calculated

Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) is the total cost of sales and marketing divided by the number of new customers acquired in the same period. It tells you how efficiently your business converts spending into customers. Lower CAC means more efficient customer acquisition.

CAC Formula

CAC = (Marketing Spend + Sales Spend) / New Customers Acquired

LTV:CAC Ratio = Customer Lifetime Value / CAC

  • Include all sales & marketing costs: ads, salaries, tools, commissions
  • A healthy LTV:CAC ratio is 3:1 or higher
  • CAC payback period = CAC / monthly revenue per customer

SaaS Company

Marketing: $15,000 + Sales: $10,000 = $25,000 total. New customers: 50.

  • CAC = $25,000 / 50 = $500 per customer
