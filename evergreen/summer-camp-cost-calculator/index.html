How Much Does Summer Camp Cost in 2026?

Summer camp prices in the United States in 2026 vary widely by format. Community and YMCA camps run $100–$300 per week, traditional day camps run $200–$500 per week, and specialty day camps for tech, sports, or arts run $500–$1,500 per week. Overnight (sleepaway) camps typically cost $800–$2,500 per week, while premium specialty residential camps can reach $1,500–$3,500 per week. Major-metro pricing runs roughly 20–30% higher than the national average, and rural areas trend 15–20% lower. A typical family sending one child to six weeks of day camp should budget $1,800–$3,600 for tuition alone before add-ons.

Dependent Care FSA vs. Child and Dependent Care Credit

Summer day camp (not overnight camp) qualifies as a dependent-care expense when it allows parents to work. A Dependent Care FSA lets you set aside up to $5,000 per household pre-tax in 2026, saving 22–37% depending on your bracket — often $1,100–$1,850 in real tax savings. If an FSA isn't available, the Child and Dependent Care Credit covers 20–35% of up to $3,000 in expenses for one child or $6,000 for two or more. Overnight camps do not qualify for either benefit, so budget accordingly. This calculator automatically picks the better of the two based on your inputs.

Tips to Reduce Summer Camp Costs

Register by February or March to lock in early-bird discounts of 5–15%. Apply for camperships and sliding-scale scholarships — most nonprofit camps (YMCA, Scouts, JCC) reserve 10–20% of spots for families needing aid. Combine a cheaper community camp for most weeks with one specialty week as a highlight. Employers sometimes offer camp subsidies or FSA matches — check your benefits portal. Siblings often get 5–10% off at the same camp. Pay with a credit card that earns category bonuses on education or childcare, and always use pre-tax FSA dollars before after-tax spending.

Planning Your 2026 Camp Budget

Start planning in January: popular camps sell out by March. Build a weekly calendar showing which weeks each child is covered, then match weeks to camp options by price. Add a 10% buffer for late-season extras like field trips, uniforms, or extended care.
