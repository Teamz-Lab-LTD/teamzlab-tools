How the Tip Calculator Works

This calculator takes your bill amount, desired tip percentage, and the number of people splitting the bill. It computes the tip amount, the total bill including tip, and each person's share. You can use preset tip percentages (10%, 15%, 18%, 20%, 25%) or enter any custom percentage.

Tipping customs vary by country and service type. In the United States, 15-20% is standard for restaurant service, while other countries may have different norms or include service charges automatically. This calculator works for any scenario where you need to calculate a tip or split a bill.

Tip Calculation Formulas

Tip Amount = Bill Amount × (Tip Percentage / 100)

Total Bill = Bill Amount + Tip Amount

Per Person = Total Bill / Number of People

Tip Per Person = Tip Amount / Number of People

Where:

  • Bill Amount = The pre-tip total on your receipt
  • Tip Percentage = How much you want to tip (typically 15-20%)
  • Number of People = How many people are splitting the bill

Example Calculations

Example 1: Dinner for Two

Bill: $85.00, Tip: 20%, People: 2

  • Tip Amount: $85 × 0.20 = $17.00
  • Total Bill: $85 + $17 = $102.00
  • Per Person: $102 / 2 = $51.00

Example 2: Group Lunch

Bill: $156.50, Tip: 18%, People: 5

  • Tip Amount: $156.50 × 0.18 = $28.17
  • Total Bill: $156.50 + $28.17 = $184.67
  • Per Person: $184.67 / 5 = $36.93

Example 3: Solo Meal

Bill: $22.50, Tip: 15%, People: 1

  • Tip Amount: $22.50 × 0.15 = $3.38
  • Total Bill: $22.50 + $3.38 = $25.88

Tipping Guidelines by Service

Standard US tipping guidelines: Restaurants (sit-down) 15-20%, Buffets 10%, Delivery 15-20% (minimum $3-5), Bartenders $1-2 per drink or 15-20% of tab, Hair stylists 15-20%, Taxi/rideshare 15-20%, Hotel housekeeping $2-5 per night, Valet $2-5. These are customary guidelines, not requirements. Always tip based on the quality of service you received.

Tipping Around the World

United States and Canada: 15-20% is expected for restaurant service. United Kingdom: 10-12.5% is common, but not obligatory if service charge is included. Europe: small rounding-up or 5-10% is typical. Japan: tipping is generally not practiced and can sometimes be considered rude. Australia: not expected but appreciated for exceptional service. Understanding local customs helps you tip appropriately when traveling.

When Service Charge Is Already Included

Many restaurants automatically add a service charge (often 10-18%) for large groups or on the bill by default. Check your receipt for lines like "service charge," "gratuity included," or "service compris" (in French). If a service charge is already included, additional tipping is optional and typically only for exceptional service.
