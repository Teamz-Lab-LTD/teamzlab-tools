How to Convert Miles to Kilometers

The mile and kilometer are both units of distance, but they belong to different measurement systems. The mile is used in the United States, United Kingdom, and a few other countries, while the kilometer is the global standard under the metric system. To convert miles to kilometers, multiply the number of miles by 1.60934. To convert kilometers to miles, divide by 1.60934 or multiply by 0.621371. This miles to km converter handles both directions instantly.

Conversion Formulas

Miles to Kilometers: miles × 1.60934 = km

Kilometers to Miles: km ÷ 1.60934 = miles

1 mile = 1.60934 km | 1 km = 0.621371 miles

Why Miles and Kilometers Exist

The mile originated from the Roman mille passus, meaning one thousand paces, which equaled roughly 5,000 Roman feet. Over centuries, different countries defined the mile differently until it was standardized at 5,280 feet (1,609.34 meters) in 1959. The kilometer was introduced during the French Revolution as part of the metric system, defined as one thousandth of the distance from the equator to the North Pole. Today, only the United States, Myanmar, and Liberia officially use miles for road distances.

Common Miles to KM Conversions

Runners often need to convert between miles and kilometers for race distances. A 5K race is 3.107 miles, a 10K is 6.214 miles, a half marathon (13.1 miles) is 21.097 km, and a full marathon (26.2 miles) is 42.195 km. For driving, common speed limits translate as follows: 30 mph equals 48.3 km/h, 55 mph equals 88.5 km/h, 65 mph equals 104.6 km/h, and 70 mph equals 112.7 km/h. These conversions are useful when traveling between countries that use different measurement systems.

Quick Estimation Trick

For a fast mental approximation, multiply miles by 1.6 or divide kilometers by 1.6. For slightly better accuracy, use the Fibonacci sequence: consecutive Fibonacci numbers approximate the mile-to-km ratio. For example, 5 miles is roughly 8 km (actual: 8.047 km), and 8 miles is roughly 13 km (actual: 12.875 km). This works because the ratio of consecutive Fibonacci numbers approaches the golden ratio (1.618), which is close to the actual conversion factor of 1.60934.
