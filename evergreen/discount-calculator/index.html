How the Discount Calculator Works

This calculator applies a percentage discount to an original price to find the sale price and your savings. You can also apply a second discount (like an additional coupon on top of a sale) and add sales tax to see the true final price you will pay.

When two discounts are applied, they are calculated sequentially — the second discount applies to the already-discounted price, not the original. This is how stores and online retailers actually apply stacked discounts.

Discount Formulas

Discount Amount = Original Price × (Discount % / 100)

Sale Price = Original Price − Discount Amount

With Second Discount: Final Price = Sale Price × (1 − Second Discount % / 100)

With Tax: Total = Final Price × (1 + Tax Rate / 100)

Example Calculations

Example 1: Simple 30% Off Sale

Original: $120.00, Discount: 30%

  • Savings: $120 × 0.30 = $36.00
  • Sale Price: $120 − $36 = $84.00

Example 2: Double Discount (Sale + Coupon)

Original: $200, First discount: 25% off, Second discount: 10% extra coupon

  • After 25%: $200 × 0.75 = $150
  • After 10%: $150 × 0.90 = $135.00
  • Total savings: $200 − $135 = $65 (32.5% total discount, not 35%)

Example 3: Discount + Sales Tax

Original: $80, Discount: 20%, Tax: 8.25%

  • Sale Price: $80 × 0.80 = $64.00
  • Tax: $64 × 0.0825 = $5.28
  • Final: $64 + $5.28 = $69.28

Why 20% Off + 10% Off Is Not 30% Off

A common misconception is that stacking discounts adds them together. In reality, the second discount applies to the already-reduced price. 20% off $100 gives $80. Then 10% off $80 gives $72 — which is 28% total, not 30%. The formula for the true combined discount is: Total Discount = 1 − (1 − D1) × (1 − D2), where D1 and D2 are the decimal discount rates.
