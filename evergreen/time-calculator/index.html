How the Time Calculator Works

This time calculator lets you add or subtract time durations expressed in hours, minutes, and seconds. It converts everything to total seconds internally, performs the arithmetic, then converts back to a readable hours:minutes:seconds format. The calculator handles overflow automatically — for example, 75 minutes becomes 1 hour and 15 minutes.

You can use this for calculating total work hours, adding up exercise durations, figuring out cooking times, computing travel durations, or any situation where you need to add or subtract time values. Enter the first time, choose add or subtract, enter the second time, and get your result instantly.

Time Calculation Method

Total Seconds = (Hours × 3600) + (Minutes × 60) + Seconds

Result = Time 1 Total Seconds ± Time 2 Total Seconds

Hours = floor(Result / 3600), Minutes = floor(Remainder / 60), Seconds = Remainder

Where:

  • 3600 = seconds in one hour
  • 60 = seconds in one minute
  • Negative results are shown with a minus sign

Example Calculations

Example 1: Add Two Work Shifts

Morning shift: 4 hours 30 minutes + Afternoon shift: 3 hours 45 minutes

  • Time 1: 4h 30m 0s = 16,200 seconds
  • Time 2: 3h 45m 0s = 13,500 seconds
  • Total: 29,700 seconds = 8 hours 15 minutes 0 seconds

Example 2: Subtract Break Time

Total shift: 8 hours 0 minutes − Lunch break: 0 hours 45 minutes

  • Time 1: 8h 0m 0s = 28,800 seconds
  • Time 2: 0h 45m 0s = 2,700 seconds
  • Result: 26,100 seconds = 7 hours 15 minutes 0 seconds

Example 3: Add Race Lap Times

Lap 1: 1 minute 32 seconds + Lap 2: 1 minute 28 seconds

  • Time 1: 0h 1m 32s = 92 seconds
  • Time 2: 0h 1m 28s = 88 seconds
  • Total: 180 seconds = 0 hours 3 minutes 0 seconds

Time Conversion Reference

For quick reference: 1 hour = 60 minutes = 3,600 seconds. 1 day = 24 hours = 1,440 minutes = 86,400 seconds. 1 week = 168 hours. When the result exceeds 24 hours, this calculator shows the total hours (e.g., 26 hours 15 minutes) rather than converting to days, which is more practical for most use cases.
