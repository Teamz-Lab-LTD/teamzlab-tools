What Is the Pomodoro Technique?

The Pomodoro Technique was created by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s. It breaks work into focused 25-minute intervals ("pomodoros") separated by short breaks. After every 4 pomodoros, you take a longer break. The method reduces burnout, improves focus, and helps you track how much focused work you do each day.

Standard Pomodoro Cycle

1 Pomodoro = 25 min work + 5 min break

4 Pomodoros = 100 min work + 15 min long break

Full cycle = ~2 hours for 100 min of focused work
