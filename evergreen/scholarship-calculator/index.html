How to Calculate Scholarship Needs

Start by adding up your total annual college costs: tuition, fees, room and board, books, and personal expenses. Then subtract any scholarships, grants, and financial aid you have been awarded. The remaining amount is your funding gap, which you will need to cover through savings, family contributions, work-study, or student loans. This calculator helps you see exactly where you stand and how much more you need to apply for.

Types of Scholarships to Apply For

Merit-based scholarships reward academic achievement, test scores, and extracurricular involvement. Need-based scholarships consider your family income and financial situation. Specialized scholarships target specific fields of study, community service, athletic ability, or demographic backgrounds. Local scholarships from community organizations often have fewer applicants and higher acceptance rates. Apply to as many as possible since multiple small scholarships can cover significant portions of your costs.

Tips to Maximize Scholarship Awards

Start applying early since many deadlines are months before the school year begins. Tailor each application to the specific scholarship criteria rather than using generic essays. Maintain a strong GPA and participate in extracurricular activities that demonstrate leadership. Check with your school financial aid office for institutional scholarships that may not be widely advertised. Reapply each year since many scholarships are renewable and new opportunities become available annually. Keep a spreadsheet tracking deadlines, requirements, and application status.
