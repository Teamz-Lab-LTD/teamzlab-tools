How the Personal Loan Calculator Works

This calculator uses the standard amortization formula to determine your fixed monthly payment (EMI) based on three inputs: the loan amount, annual interest rate, and repayment term. The formula ensures each payment covers both interest charges and principal repayment, with the split shifting gradually over time.

EMI Formula

EMI = P × [r(1+r)n] / [(1+r)n − 1]

Where: P = loan principal, r = monthly interest rate (APR ÷ 12 ÷ 100), n = total number of monthly payments

In the early months, a larger portion of your payment goes toward interest. As the outstanding balance decreases, more of each payment reduces the principal. The amortization schedule shows this breakdown month by month.

Personal Loan vs Other Loan Types

Personal loans are unsecured, meaning no collateral is required. This makes approval faster but typically results in higher interest rates compared to secured loans. Here is how they compare:

  • Personal loans: 2-7 year terms, 6-36% APR, unsecured, fixed payments
  • Auto loans: 3-7 years, 4-10% APR, secured by the vehicle
  • Mortgage: 15-30 years, 3-8% APR, secured by property
  • Credit cards: Revolving, 15-30% APR, minimum payments only

Personal loans work best for debt consolidation, home improvements, medical expenses, or major purchases where you want a fixed payment schedule and defined payoff date.

How to Get the Best Personal Loan Rate

Your interest rate depends heavily on your credit score, income, and debt-to-income ratio. To secure the lowest rate: check and improve your credit score before applying, compare offers from at least three lenders, consider credit unions which often offer lower rates, and choose the shortest term you can comfortably afford. Pre-qualification with a soft credit check lets you compare rates without impacting your score.

Understanding APR and Interest Rates

The APR (Annual Percentage Rate) includes the base interest rate plus origination fees and other costs, expressed as a yearly rate. A loan advertised at 8% interest with a 3% origination fee has a higher effective APR. Always compare APR, not just interest rates, when shopping for loans. This calculator uses APR for accurate total cost projections.
