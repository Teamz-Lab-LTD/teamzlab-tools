Add each family member, then list their clothing items with quantities and prices. The planner calculates per-person costs and a grand total, showing how much of your budget remains. Add items like shalwar kameez, saree, shoes, jewelry, mehndi, tailoring, and accessories for each person. Keep your Eid fashion spending organized and avoid overspending.

Eid Fashion Tips on a Budget

You do not need to buy everything new. Mix new pieces with existing wardrobe items. Shop early to avoid price hikes closer to Eid. Compare prices online before visiting stores. Consider sharing tailors with family to negotiate better rates. Buy fabric and get it tailored instead of ready-made for a custom fit at a lower price.

Eid Shopping Timeline

Start shopping 3-4 weeks before Eid for the best selection and prices. Order online items at least 2 weeks early to allow for shipping. Book tailoring appointments early — tailors get extremely busy in the last week before Eid. Shoes and accessories can be bought last as they require less planning.

Family Budget Planning for Eid

Set a total Eid outfit budget first, then allocate per person based on need. Children grow fast and need new clothes more than adults. Prioritize the main Eid day outfit and consider simpler options for secondary gatherings. Using this planner helps prevent impulse purchases and ensures everyone gets something nice within budget. Track every expense — from the main outfit to small accessories — so there are no surprises on Eid day. A well-planned budget means you can enjoy the festive shopping experience without financial stress afterwards.
