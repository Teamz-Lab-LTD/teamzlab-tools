What Is Eid Salami?

Eid Salami is the cherished tradition of giving monetary gifts during Eid celebrations, practiced across Muslim communities worldwide. The word "Salami" derives from the Arabic greeting of peace (Salam) and is particularly popular in Bangladesh, where elders present crisp banknotes to children and younger family members as a token of love, blessing, and celebration. In other regions, this tradition goes by names like Eidi, Eidiya, or Eid gift, but the spirit remains the same: generosity, joy, and family bonds strengthened through giving.

Tips for Giving Eid Salami

  • Present Salami in crisp new banknotes or a decorated envelope for a special touch.
  • Give with a warm smile and Eid greeting — the gesture matters as much as the amount.
  • Be consistent with amounts among children of the same age to avoid comparisons.
  • Pair your digital Salami card with the physical gift for a memorable experience.
  • Share the card on WhatsApp first, then hand over the cash in person for double delight.
