How the Eid Political Poster Maker Works

This free Eid political poster maker lets you create professional greeting posters in the style used by politicians, community leaders, and organizations during Eid-ul-Fitr and Eid-ul-Adha. Upload your main photo which appears large in the center, add two smaller leader or elder photos at the top corners, customize every line of text including the Bismillah header, main greeting, body message, your name, and designation. The poster renders live on an HTML5 canvas as you type and is ready to download as a high-resolution PNG for social media sharing or printing.

Everything runs entirely in your browser. No photos are uploaded to any server, no account is needed, and your personal information stays completely private on your device. Choose from eight professionally designed themes featuring Islamic decorative elements like crescent moons, stars, lanterns, and mosque silhouettes.

Who Uses Political Eid Posters

Political-style Eid greeting posters are a tradition across Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, and the Middle East. Ward commissioners, union parishad chairmen, party leaders, MPs, councillors, club presidents, mosque committee members, business owners, NGO heads, and social workers share personalized Eid cards with their communities every year. The poster features a prominent name banner at the bottom with designation, just like the printed posters and banners displayed on streets and shared on Facebook and WhatsApp groups during Eid season. This tool lets anyone create the same professional result without needing a graphic designer.

Customization and Text Options

Every element on the poster is fully editable. The top line defaults to Bismillah but can be changed to any slogan or organization motto. Left and right tags above the leader photos can show party name, location, or any label. The main greeting supports Bengali, Arabic, Urdu, Hindi, Malay, English, or any Unicode text. The body message area holds your personal Eid greeting. The name banner at the bottom comes in six color options and displays your name prominently with your full designation below. Three size options cover square posts, portrait format, and vertical story format for different social media platforms.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I make a political Eid poster?

Upload your main photo, optionally add two leader photos, fill in your name and designation, customize the greeting texts, choose a theme and banner color, then download or share. The poster preview updates live as you edit.

What photos should I upload?

Your main photo appears large in the center of the poster. The left and right photos are for party leaders, family elders, or organization founders and appear as smaller circles at the top. All photos are automatically cropped to circles.

Can I write in Bengali or Arabic?

Yes. All text fields support Bengali, Arabic, Urdu, Hindi, and any Unicode language. The default texts are in Bengali matching the popular political poster style used in Bangladesh.

What sizes are available?

Square 1080 by 1080 for social posts, portrait 1080 by 1350 for Facebook, and story 1080 by 1920 for Instagram and WhatsApp stories. All produce sharp high-resolution images.

Is my data private?

Everything runs in your browser using Canvas API. No photos or text are sent to any server. Your images stay on your device unless you download and share the poster yourself.
