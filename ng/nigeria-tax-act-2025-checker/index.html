Overview of the Nigeria Tax Reform Act 2025

The Nigeria Tax Reform Act 2025 represents one of the most significant overhauls of the Nigerian tax system in decades. The reform aims to simplify the tax code, broaden the tax base, improve revenue collection, and make Nigeria more competitive for businesses. Key changes include adjustments to corporate income tax (CIT) rates, modifications to the VAT regime including rate changes and expanded exemptions, updates to personal income tax brackets, and improved provisions for small businesses. The reform also introduces new digital economy taxation provisions targeting technology companies and platforms operating in Nigeria.

Impact on Different Business Types

The reform affects different business types differently. Companies (limited liability corporations) face changes in CIT rates and filing requirements. Sole proprietors may benefit from simplified tax regimes for small businesses with turnover below certain thresholds. Partnerships have specific provisions regarding the taxation of partnership income distributed to partners. NGOs and not-for-profit organisations may qualify for exemptions on specific income types but still face compliance requirements. Understanding which provisions apply to your specific business structure is the first step to compliance.

Key Thresholds in the 2025 Tax Act

  • Small Business Relief: Turnover below ₦25 million
  • Medium Business: Turnover ₦25 million – ₦100 million
  • Large Business: Turnover above ₦100 million
  • VAT Registration Threshold: ₦25 million annual turnover
  • CIT Rate: 30% (large), 20% (medium), 0% (small eligible)

Compliance Timeline

Businesses should begin preparing for compliance immediately. Key dates include the effective date of the Act, filing deadlines for updated returns, and transition periods for specific provisions. The Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) has published implementation guidelines with detailed timelines. Early preparation is advisable to avoid penalties and ensure a smooth transition to the new requirements. Consult with a tax professional to develop a compliance roadmap specific to your business.
