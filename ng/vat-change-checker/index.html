Nigeria VAT System Overview

The Value Added Tax (VAT) in Nigeria is a consumption tax levied on the supply of goods and services. The current standard VAT rate is 7.5%, which was increased from 5% in February 2020. VAT is administered by the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS) and applies to most goods and services supplied in Nigeria, with specific exemptions for essential items. The 2025 tax reform has introduced additional changes to the VAT framework including expanded exemptions, updated registration thresholds, and new provisions for digital services.

VAT-Exempt Goods and Services

Certain goods and services are exempt from VAT in Nigeria. These include basic food items (unprocessed agricultural produce, raw foodstuffs), medical and pharmaceutical products, educational materials and services, baby products, exported goods (zero-rated), and certain financial services. The 2025 reform has expanded the exempt list to include additional essential items aimed at reducing the cost of living for ordinary Nigerians. Businesses that exclusively sell exempt goods may not need to register for VAT but should still maintain records and file nil returns if registered.

Key VAT Numbers

  • Standard VAT Rate: 7.5%
  • Registration Threshold: ₦25 million annual turnover
  • Filing Frequency: Monthly (by 21st of following month)
  • Exempt Categories: Basic food, medical, education, baby products
  • Zero-Rated: Exports, diplomatic supplies

VAT Compliance Requirements

VAT-registered businesses in Nigeria must charge VAT on taxable supplies, issue VAT-compliant invoices, file monthly VAT returns by the 21st of the following month, remit collected VAT to FIRS, maintain records for at least six years, and submit annual VAT returns. Non-compliance can result in penalties including fines, interest on unpaid VAT, and potential prosecution. The FIRS has been increasing enforcement through technology, including cross-referencing VAT returns with bank transactions and third-party data. Businesses should ensure their VAT compliance is up to date.
