How to Measure for a Sports Jersey

Getting the right jersey size starts with an accurate chest measurement. Use a flexible measuring tape and wrap it around the fullest part of your chest, just under your armpits. Keep the tape snug but not tight, and make sure it stays level all the way around your back. Stand naturally and breathe normally while measuring. Write down the number in inches or centimeters. If you are between two sizes, the fit preference toggle above helps you decide — choose "tight" for a game-day athletic look, "regular" for everyday wear, or "loose" for layering over hoodies and casual comfort. Women should measure across the fullest part of the bust, and youth sizing typically corresponds to age and chest measurements combined.

Authentic vs Replica vs Limited Jerseys

Sports jersey types differ in construction, fit, and price. Authentic jerseys are identical to what players wear on the field or court. They use vapor-knit or elite fabrics, feature stitched-on tackle twill numbers, and have a tailored athletic cut that runs slimmer than consumer sizes. Replica jerseys (also called "Game" jerseys in Nike's NFL line) are designed for fans. They use screen-printed numbers, looser relaxed-fit cuts, and breathable but standard polyester fabric. They are the most affordable option and the most popular. Limited jerseys sit between authentic and replica — they feature twill numbers like authentics but use a standard consumer fit like replicas. When choosing between types, remember that authentic jerseys tend to run one size smaller than replicas, so size up if you prefer an authentic with a relaxed feel. This guide provides sizing for the standard consumer (replica/limited) fit, which is what most fans purchase.

Sizing Tips for Each League

Each league has slightly different sizing conventions. NFL jerseys tend to run large because they are designed to accommodate shoulder pads underneath for on-field use, so fans often find their regular size comfortable even in a replica. NBA jerseys are sleeveless and designed for freedom of movement, so they have wider armholes and a boxier cut — if you want a closer fit, consider sizing down. MLB jerseys use a button-front design similar to dress shirts, so chest measurement is the primary factor. NHL jerseys are the roomiest of all because they are traditionally worn over hockey pads, making them ideal for layering. Soccer jerseys run the tightest of all major sports leagues because they are designed for aerobic performance with no equipment underneath — if you are used to American sizing, you may want to size up one for soccer kits. Weight, height, and personal preference all play a role, but chest circumference remains the single most reliable measurement for finding your size across all leagues.
