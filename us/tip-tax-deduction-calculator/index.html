How the New Tip Tax Deduction Works

Starting with the 2025 tax year, workers who receive qualified cash tips can deduct up to $25,000 of that tip income from their federal taxes. This applies to both W-2 employees and self-employed workers like Uber/Lyft drivers. The deduction phases out at higher income levels.

Tip Deduction Rules and Limits

Quick Reference (2025–2028 Tax Years)

  • Maximum deduction: $25,000 per year
  • Applies to: qualified cash tips reported on W-2 or Schedule SE
  • Phase-out starts: $150,000 MAGI (single) / $300,000 (married filing jointly)
  • Fully phases out: $400,000 MAGI (single) / $550,000 (married filing jointly)
  • Available for: tax years 2025 through 2028

Who Qualifies?

You qualify if you receive cash tips as part of your regular income. This includes restaurant servers, bartenders, delivery drivers, hair stylists, hotel staff, rideshare drivers, and other service workers who receive tips. The tips must be reported on your tax return.

Example

Server earning $45,000 + $20,000 in tips

  • Tip deduction: $20,000 (under the $25,000 cap)
  • At 22% marginal rate: $4,400 tax savings
  • Effective tip tax rate: $0 (tips are tax-free up to $25K)
