How to Build a Strong Resume

A well-crafted resume is the single most important document in your job search. It serves as your first impression with hiring managers and determines whether you get an interview. The key to an effective resume is presenting your experience and skills in a clear, concise, and professional format that immediately communicates your value. Start with a strong professional summary that highlights your most relevant experience and career goals in two to three sentences. This section sits at the top of your resume and sets the tone for everything that follows. Use active language and include specific metrics wherever possible, such as "increased revenue by 30%" or "managed a team of 12 engineers."

ATS Optimization Tips for Your Resume

Applicant Tracking Systems scan resumes for keywords that match the job description. To maximize your chances of passing ATS screening, use standard section headings like "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills." Avoid tables, columns in PDF format, and graphics that ATS parsers cannot read. Include both the full term and its abbreviation when listing technical skills, for example "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)." Match the exact language used in the job posting. If the listing says "project management," use that phrase rather than "managed projects." Our templates are designed with ATS compatibility in mind, using clean formatting and standard headings that every major ATS platform can parse correctly.

Quantify your achievements wherever possible. Instead of writing "responsible for sales," write "generated $2.4M in annual revenue across 50+ enterprise accounts." Numbers stand out to both ATS systems and human recruiters, making your accomplishments concrete and measurable. Focus on results rather than duties, and start each bullet point with a strong action verb like "developed," "implemented," "optimized," or "led."

What to Include in Each Resume Section

Your personal information should include your full name, professional email address, phone number, and city or state. Adding a LinkedIn URL is highly recommended as recruiters frequently cross-reference profiles. A personal website or portfolio link is valuable for creative and technical roles. The work experience section should list positions in reverse chronological order, with three to five bullet points per role describing your key achievements and responsibilities. Use the STAR method: describe the Situation, Task, Action, and Result for each accomplishment.

The education section should include your degree, institution name, and graduation year. Including your GPA is optional and generally recommended only if it is above 3.5 or if you are a recent graduate. The skills section should list your most relevant technical and professional skills. Use the tag input in this builder to add skills individually, making it easy to customize for each application. Languages are increasingly valuable in a global job market, so include any languages you speak along with your proficiency level. A complete resume with all sections filled out demonstrates thoroughness and professionalism to potential employers.
