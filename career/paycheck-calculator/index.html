How Paycheck Calculations Work Globally

A paycheck calculator converts your gross pay — the headline number on your offer letter or hourly rate — into the actual net amount that lands in your bank account after taxes and mandatory contributions. Every country uses a slightly different mix of income tax, social insurance, and regional levies, which is why a single global rate-of-thumb never matches reality. This calculator applies the current official bracket structure for the United States, United Kingdom, India, Australia, and Canada, then converts the result into weekly, monthly, and annual take-home views so you can budget against whichever pay cycle your employer uses. Last updated: 2026.

Gross pay is what the employer agreed to pay you before any deductions. Net pay is what you can actually spend. Between the two sit progressive income tax brackets, mandatory social contributions (Social Security, National Insurance, superannuation, CPP/EI), and — in federal countries like the US and Canada — an additional regional tax layer. For most full-time workers, the gap between gross and net sits between 20% and 40% of gross pay, which is why comparing job offers or planning a household budget without running the numbers for your specific country is almost always misleading.

Country-by-Country Tax Rules

United States (2026 estimates): Federal income tax uses progressive brackets from 10% to 37%, plus FICA payroll tax at 7.65% (6.2% Social Security on earnings up to the wage base, 1.45% Medicare on all earnings). State tax ranges from 0% in Texas, Florida, and Washington to roughly 13% in California. This tool asks you to pick a state-tax tier (none, low, medium, or high) rather than modelling every state rule — for state-precise figures, use the US-only version linked in the results.

United Kingdom: Income tax applies at 20% between the £12,570 personal allowance and £50,270, 40% between £50,270 and £125,140, and 45% above that. National Insurance is 12% on earnings between £12,570 and £50,270, and 2% above that. Scotland uses different bands and is approximated here.

India (new regime, FY 2025-26): Slabs of 0%, 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, and 30% apply to income above ₹3 lakh, with a full rebate up to ₹7 lakh under section 87A. Professional tax and cess are simplified in this estimate.

Australia (2025-26): Resident brackets run from 0% below A$18,200 to 45% above A$190,000, plus a 2% Medicare levy on most incomes. Superannuation is employer-paid (11.5%) and is not deducted from your gross here.

Canada: Federal brackets from 15% to 33%, plus a flat provincial tier you select (Ontario, BC, Alberta, or Quebec). CPP and EI contributions are approximated as a combined 7.5% for simplicity.

Pre-Tax Deductions and Take-Home Impact

Pre-tax deductions reduce your taxable income before income tax is calculated. In the US that typically means 401(k) contributions and traditional HSA/FSA contributions; in the UK, a workplace pension under salary sacrifice; in India, deductions under sections 80C and 80D if you opt for the old regime; in Australia, salary-sacrificed superannuation; in Canada, RRSP contributions made through payroll. Because every dollar put into a pre-tax bucket skips both federal and regional income tax, the effective cost of saving is lower than it appears — contributing $500 a month to a 401(k) in the 22% federal tax bracket only reduces take-home pay by roughly $390. Hourly, Weekly, Monthly — Converting Pay Periods

Your annual take-home pay is fixed by your salary and tax situation; pay frequency only changes how it is distributed across the year. Standard conversions: hourly pay assumes 52 weeks × your hours per week; weekly pay divides annual by 52; biweekly by 26; semi-monthly by 24; monthly by 12. Biweekly earners receive three paychecks twice a year, which is a useful budgeting cushion, while semi-monthly earners always receive two paychecks per month. This calculator computes the full annual figure internally and then shows the per-period net pay in the exact frequency you selected, plus weekly/monthly/annual for easy budget comparison.
