What Is White Noise?

White noise is a consistent sound that contains every frequency across the audible spectrum at equal intensity. It sounds like a steady hiss — similar to a TV tuned to an empty channel or the rush of a fan. White noise is widely used for sleep, focus, and sound masking because it effectively drowns out sudden changes in background noise that can wake you up or break concentration.

Types of Noise Explained

White noise has equal energy at every frequency, producing a bright, hissy sound. Pink noise reduces higher frequencies, creating a deeper, more balanced sound — like steady rainfall. It is often considered more natural and soothing. Brown noise (also called Brownian or red noise) further emphasizes low frequencies, producing a deep rumble like thunder or a strong waterfall. Many people find brown noise the most relaxing of the three.

Science of Sound Masking

Research shows that consistent background noise helps mask sudden auditory disruptions — a door slamming, a car horn, a notification ding — that pull your attention away from sleep or deep work. A 2012 study in the Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience found that moderate ambient noise (around 70 dB) can enhance creative thinking. Meanwhile, a 2017 study in Frontiers in Human Neuroscience demonstrated that pink noise during sleep improved memory consolidation in older adults.

Benefits for Focus and Sleep

For focus, noise generators create a consistent auditory environment that reduces the brain's tendency to seek out novel sounds. This is especially beneficial in open offices or noisy environments. For sleep, white and pink noise can shorten the time it takes to fall asleep, reduce nighttime awakenings, and improve overall sleep quality. Brown noise, with its deep tones, is particularly effective for people who find higher-frequency sounds irritating.
