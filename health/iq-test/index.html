What Does an IQ Score Measure?

IQ (Intelligence Quotient) measures cognitive abilities relative to the general population. It assesses fluid intelligence — your ability to reason, recognize patterns, and solve novel problems — rather than crystallized intelligence (learned knowledge). Modern IQ tests like the Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale (WAIS) and Stanford-Binet measure multiple cognitive domains rather than a single "intelligence" number.

IQ Test Categories Explained

Pattern Recognition: Identifying rules and sequences in visual or numerical patterns. This is often considered the purest measure of fluid intelligence. Logical Reasoning: Deductive and inductive reasoning through word problems and syllogisms. Mathematical Ability: Numerical computation, arithmetic reasoning, and quantitative problem-solving. Working Memory: Holding and manipulating information in your mind. Spatial Reasoning: Mental rotation, visualization, and understanding spatial relationships.

Is This IQ Test Accurate?

Online IQ tests provide an estimate, not a clinical measurement. Professional IQ tests (WAIS-IV, Stanford-Binet 5) are administered by psychologists over 1-2 hours and provide the most accurate results. This test gives a reasonable approximation based on well-established cognitive challenge types. For an official IQ assessment, consult a licensed psychologist.

Tips for Taking This Assessment

Answer each question honestly based on your real behavior and preferences, not what you think the ideal answer should be. Go with your first instinct rather than overthinking — spontaneous answers tend to be more accurate. Take the assessment in a quiet environment where you can focus without distractions. Remember that there are no right or wrong answers. Your results are meant to provide self-awareness and starting points for improvement, not definitive labels.
