How Semaglutide Dosing Works

Semaglutide is a GLP-1 receptor agonist prescribed for type 2 diabetes (Ozempic) and weight management (Wegovy). It is injected subcutaneously once weekly. The dose is gradually increased over several months through a process called titration, which allows your body to adjust and minimizes gastrointestinal side effects like nausea. Understanding how to convert your prescribed milligram dose into the correct syringe units is essential for safe self-injection, especially when using compounded semaglutide from a vial rather than a pre-filled pen.

Understanding Titration Schedules

The standard semaglutide titration schedule starts at 0.25 mg per week for the first four weeks, then increases to 0.5 mg for weeks five through eight. After that, the dose increases every four weeks: 1 mg for weeks nine through twelve, 1.7 mg for weeks thirteen through sixteen, and finally the maintenance dose of 2.4 mg from week seventeen onward. Your prescriber may modify this schedule based on your tolerance and treatment goals. For Ozempic (diabetes), the maximum dose is typically 2 mg. For Wegovy (weight loss), the target maintenance dose is 2.4 mg. Never increase your dose faster than prescribed, as this significantly increases the risk of side effects.

Converting Milligrams to Syringe Units

When using compounded semaglutide from a vial, you need to calculate how much liquid to draw into your syringe. The formula is straightforward: divide your prescribed dose in milligrams by the vial concentration in mg/mL to get the volume in milliliters. Then multiply by the syringe capacity to get the number of units to draw. For example, if your dose is 0.5 mg and your vial is 2.5 mg/mL, you need 0.2 mL, which is 20 units on a 100-unit (1 mL) syringe. Different syringe sizes have different unit markings: a 0.3 mL syringe has 30 units, a 0.5 mL syringe has 50 units, and a 1 mL syringe has 100 units. Always use the smallest syringe that accommodates your volume for the most accurate measurement.

Weight Loss vs Diabetes Dosing

Semaglutide is marketed under different brand names for different conditions. Ozempic is approved for type 2 diabetes at doses up to 2 mg weekly, while Wegovy is approved for chronic weight management at a maintenance dose of 2.4 mg weekly. Compounded semaglutide is available from compounding pharmacies at various concentrations. Regardless of the source, the titration principle remains the same: start low, increase gradually, and reach your target maintenance dose over 16-20 weeks.
