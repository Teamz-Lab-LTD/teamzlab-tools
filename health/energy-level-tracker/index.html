Why Track Your Energy Levels?

Everyone has natural energy fluctuations throughout the day, yet most people schedule their hardest work randomly. By tracking your energy levels, you can identify your peak hours — when you are sharpest and most focused — and align your most important tasks with those windows. This simple practice can dramatically increase productivity without adding more hours to your day.

Understanding Chronotypes

Your chronotype is your natural tendency toward being a morning person or an evening person. Dr. Michael Breus identifies four chronotypes: Lions (early risers who peak in the morning), Bears (who follow the solar cycle), Wolves (who come alive in the evening), and Dolphins (light sleepers with irregular patterns). Knowing your chronotype helps you design a schedule that works with your biology instead of against it.

Ultradian Rhythms

Beyond the circadian rhythm (your 24-hour cycle), your body follows 90-120 minute ultradian rhythms throughout the day. These cycles alternate between high and low alertness. The most productive approach is to work in 90-minute focused blocks during high-energy periods and take breaks during natural energy dips. This tracker helps you identify these patterns.

Matching Work to Energy

Schedule your most demanding cognitive tasks — deep work, creative projects, complex problem-solving — during your peak energy hours. Save routine tasks — email, admin, scheduling — for low-energy periods. Physical exercise is best during moderate energy periods. Critical decisions should never be made during energy troughs.

How to Use This Tracker

Log your energy level (1-5) for each hour you are awake. Do this for at least 3 days — ideally a full week — to see reliable patterns. The chart will show your average energy curve, highlighting your peak and trough hours. Use these insights to restructure your daily schedule around your biology.

Tips for Effective Tracking

Consistency is more important than perfection — update your tracker regularly even if the data is not perfect. Set a daily reminder to log your entries while they are fresh. Review your progress weekly to spot patterns and make adjustments. Your data is saved automatically in your browser, so you do not need to worry about losing it between sessions. Export or screenshot your data periodically as a backup.
