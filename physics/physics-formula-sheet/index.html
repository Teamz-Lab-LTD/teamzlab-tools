Physics Formula Categories Explained

Mechanics covers forces, motion, energy, and momentum — the foundation of classical physics. Waves includes wave speed, frequency, period, and refraction. Electricity spans Ohm's law, power, circuits, and Coulomb's force. Optics deals with lenses, mirrors, and magnification. Thermodynamics covers heat transfer, gas laws, and efficiency. Nuclear physics includes mass-energy equivalence, radioactive decay, and decay constants.

Understanding which category a formula belongs to helps you organise your revision and quickly locate the right equation during exams. Many physics problems require combining formulas from different categories — for example, calculating the electrical energy consumed by a resistor involves both electricity and energy equations.

Study Tips for Memorising Physics Formulas

The best way to memorise physics formulas is through active practice, not passive reading. Solve problems using each formula repeatedly until the equation becomes second nature. Create flashcards with the formula on one side and a sample problem on the other. Group related formulas together — for example, the three SUVAT equations all describe motion under constant acceleration.

Focus on understanding the physics behind each formula rather than rote memorisation. If you understand that kinetic energy depends on mass and the square of velocity, you will naturally remember KE = ½mv². Use dimensional analysis to verify your formulas — if the units do not match, the formula is wrong. This sheet gives you the variable units for every equation to help with that check.
