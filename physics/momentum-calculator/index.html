Conservation of Momentum in Collisions

The law of conservation of momentum states that the total momentum of a closed system remains constant if no external forces act on it. This principle is the foundation for solving collision problems in physics. Understanding momentum conservation allows engineers to design safer vehicles, physicists to predict particle behavior, and sports scientists to analyze impact forces.
