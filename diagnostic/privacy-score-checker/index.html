What Is a Privacy Score?

Your privacy score represents how well your browser protects you from online tracking and identification. A higher score means your browser leaks less information and is harder for websites, advertisers, and trackers to identify and follow. The score is based on multiple factors including VPN usage, leak prevention, tracking protection settings, and browser configuration.

What We Test

  • VPN / Proxy Detection — Whether your IP is associated with a VPN, proxy, or datacenter, hiding your real location.
  • WebRTC Leak Prevention — Whether your browser blocks WebRTC from revealing your real IP address.
  • Do Not Track — Whether your browser sends the DNT header to request websites not track you.
  • Cookie Settings — Whether cookies and third-party storage are enabled, which can be used for tracking.
  • Canvas Fingerprint Resistance — Whether your browser protects against canvas-based fingerprinting techniques.
  • WebGL Information — How much graphics hardware information your browser exposes.
  • Connection Security — Whether you are accessing this page over a secure HTTPS connection.
  • Browser API Exposure — How many privacy-sensitive APIs your browser makes available to websites.

How to Improve Your Privacy Score

The most impactful step is using a reputable VPN, which hides your IP and encrypts your traffic. Beyond that, use a privacy-focused browser like Firefox or Brave, install extensions like uBlock Origin and Privacy Badger, disable WebRTC, enable Do Not Track, and regularly clear cookies and site data.

Why Does Privacy Matter?

  • Websites and advertisers build detailed profiles about your interests, location, and habits
  • Data brokers sell your browsing data to third parties without your knowledge
  • Browser fingerprinting can track you even without cookies
  • Your ISP can see every website you visit without a VPN
  • Public WiFi networks expose your traffic to anyone nearby
