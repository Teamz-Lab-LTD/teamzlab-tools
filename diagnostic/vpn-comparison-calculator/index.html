How to Choose the Best VPN

Choosing a VPN is not just about price. While this calculator helps you compare costs, you should also consider speed, server locations, privacy policy (no-logs), device support, and extra features like ad blocking or split tunneling. The cheapest VPN is not always the best value if it sacrifices performance or security.

Key Factors Beyond Price

  • Speed — Faster VPNs use WireGuard protocol and have extensive server networks. Speed varies by provider.
  • Privacy Policy — Look for independently audited no-logs policies. Your VPN should not track your activity.
  • Server Locations — More countries and cities mean better options for bypassing geo-restrictions and finding fast servers.
  • Simultaneous Connections — Some VPNs allow unlimited devices, others limit to 5-10.
  • Money-Back Guarantee — Most VPNs offer 30-day guarantees. Test before committing to a long plan.

Monthly vs Yearly vs 2-Year Plans

VPN pricing follows a simple pattern: longer commitments cost less per month. A monthly plan gives you flexibility but costs 2-5x more per month than a 2-year plan. If you are confident in a provider, the 2-year plan offers the best value. If you are trying a new VPN, start with a monthly plan or use the money-back guarantee on a longer plan.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

  • Renewal prices are often higher than the introductory rate
  • Some VPNs charge extra for dedicated IP addresses
  • Ad blockers, password managers, and cloud storage may be paid add-ons
