How Does the VPN Speed Test Work?

This speed test measures three key metrics of your internet connection. Download speed is measured by fetching a known file from a CDN multiple times and calculating the transfer rate. Latency is measured by timing round-trip requests to DNS resolvers. Jitter is calculated from the variation between latency measurements. These are the same metrics that affect your VPN experience.

What Each Metric Means

  • Download Speed (Mbps) — How fast data travels to your device. Higher is better. VPNs typically reduce speed by 10-30% due to encryption overhead and routing.
  • Latency (ms) — The time for a round-trip request. Lower is better. VPNs add latency because traffic routes through the VPN server. Under 50ms is excellent, 50-100ms is good, over 100ms may feel slow.
  • Jitter (ms) — Variation in latency between requests. Lower is better. High jitter causes stuttering in video calls and gaming. Under 10ms is excellent, 10-30ms is acceptable.

Why Is My VPN Slow?

VPN speed depends on several factors: the distance to the VPN server (closer servers are faster), the VPN protocol used (WireGuard is fastest, OpenVPN is slower), server load (popular servers get congested), your base internet speed, and your VPN provider's infrastructure quality.

Tips to Improve VPN Speed

  • Connect to the nearest VPN server to your physical location
  • Use WireGuard protocol if your VPN supports it — it is significantly faster than OpenVPN
  • Try different servers — some may be less congested than others
  • Use a wired connection instead of WiFi for more consistent speeds
  • Close background apps that consume bandwidth
  • Upgrade your VPN plan if your provider offers faster tiers
