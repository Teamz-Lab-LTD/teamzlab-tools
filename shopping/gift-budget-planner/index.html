How to Plan Your Gift Budget

The average American spends over $900 on holiday gifts alone, and many go into debt doing so. By planning ahead and setting a budget for each person on your list, you can give thoughtful gifts without financial stress. This planner helps you track spending across all occasions throughout the year.

The Monthly Savings Approach

Instead of scrambling for gift money in December, divide your total annual gift budget by 12 and save that amount each month. If your total gift spending is $1,200 per year, that is just $100 per month. By the time the holidays arrive, the money is already set aside.

Budget Formulas

Total Gift Budget = Sum of All Person Budgets

Monthly Savings = Total Budget ÷ Months Until Next Occasion

Remaining = Total Budget − Already Purchased

Thoughtful Cheap Gift Ideas

The best gifts do not have to be expensive. Handwritten letters, homemade food, photo albums, curated playlists, a day of help with a project, or teaching someone a skill you know are all deeply personal and cost very little. Studies show recipients value thoughtfulness and effort over price.

Experience Gifts vs Material Gifts

Research from Cornell University shows that experiences create more lasting happiness than material goods. Concert tickets, cooking classes, escape rooms, spa days, and travel vouchers are often more appreciated than physical items. They also do not add clutter to someone's home.

Gift Budgeting Tips

Set a firm total budget and stick to it. Use the gift ideas section to find options within each range. Buy gifts throughout the year when you see good deals. Consider group gifts for expensive items. Agree on spending limits with family. Remember that your presence and effort matter more than the price tag.
