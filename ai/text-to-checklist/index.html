Text to Checklist

Convert any paragraph text into a clean, bulleted checklist. Uses Chrome's built-in AI to extract key points. Runs on your device — nothing is sent anywhere.

This tool uses Chrome's built-in Summarizer API (Gemini Nano) with type=key-points. It runs 100% on your device for complete privacy. Requires Chrome 138+ with AI features enabled.
This tool works in three modes: AI-powered (Chrome 138+), Transformers.js (any browser, downloads once), and smart analysis (instant, all browsers). Learn more.

How the Text to Checklist Tool Works

This tool uses Chrome's built-in Summarizer API with the key-points type to extract the most important items from your text and present them as a clickable checklist. The AI understands context and groups related items intelligently. When Chrome AI is unavailable, the tool splits text on sentence boundaries, detects existing bullet points and numbered lists, and creates a checklist item for each distinct point. Paste paragraphs, instructions, requirements, or meeting notes and get an organized checklist in seconds.

Best Use Cases for Text to Checklist

This tool works well across many scenarios. Turn written instructions into step-by-step checklists that are easy to follow and check off. Extract requirements from project briefs or specification documents into individual checkable items. Pull action items from meeting notes into a clear to-do list with nothing missed. Convert recipe paragraphs into ingredient and step lists. Any text containing multiple items, tasks, or points can become an organized checklist that helps you track progress and stay on top of what needs to happen next.

Private Checklist Generation

Your text is processed entirely on your device and nothing is uploaded to any server. Chrome runs the Gemini Nano AI model directly on your hardware for complete privacy. The sentence-based fallback also runs entirely in JavaScript within your browser. This makes the tool safe for converting confidential project requirements, internal process documents, or private notes into checklists without exposing sensitive content to third-party services.
