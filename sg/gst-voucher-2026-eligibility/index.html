What Is the GST Voucher Scheme

The GST Voucher (GSTV) is a permanent Singapore Government scheme launched in 2012 to help lower- and middle-income Singaporeans offset their Goods and Services Tax expenses. It is funded by the GST Voucher Fund and disbursed annually in four components — Cash, MediSave, U-Save and Service & Conservancy Charges (S&CC) rebates — based on citizenship, age, assessable income from your IRAS Notice of Assessment, and the Annual Value of your home.

For 2026, eligible Singapore Citizens can receive up to S$850 in GSTV-Cash, S$150 to S$450 in MediSave top-ups, U-Save rebates credited quarterly to the utilities bill, and S&CC rebates applied directly to HDB conservancy charges. Only Singapore Citizens qualify — Permanent Residents and foreigners are not eligible for any GSTV component.

Cash vs MediSave vs U-Save vs S&CC

GSTV-Cash pays up to S$850 if your Annual Value is S$21,000 or below, or S$450 if the AV is between S$21,001 and S$25,000. Assessable income must not exceed S$34,000 and you must be aged 21 or above. GSTV-MediSave is credited to your CPF MediSave Account and ranges from S$150 (ages 65-74) to S$450 (ages 85+), regardless of the cash-tier threshold so long as AV is within limits.

GSTV-U-Save is a utility rebate paid quarterly (January, April, July, October) only to HDB households, ranging from about S$220 to S$760 annually depending on flat size. GSTV-S&CC rebates cover 1.5 to 3.5 months of S&CC charges each year, again only for HDB households. Private-property owners are not eligible for U-Save or S&CC.

2026 Payout Schedule

GSTV-Cash is typically disbursed in early August 2026 via PayNow-NRIC, bank credit or GovCash. GSTV-MediSave is credited to MediSave in the same August window. GSTV-U-Save is credited to the household utilities account in four tranches — January, April, July and October 2026. GSTV-S&CC is applied to conservancy bills in January, April, July and October. If you missed previous years, the Assurance Package top-ups may also be bundled with your payout depending on Budget 2026 announcements.

How to Sign Up if You Missed It

Most eligible Singaporeans are automatically enrolled and receive the payout without action. If you have not signed up before, visit gstvoucher.gov.sg and link PayNow to your NRIC for the fastest crediting. You can also use the GSTV e-service on Singpass to check eligibility, update bank details, or request a GovCash cheque. Last updated: April 2026.
