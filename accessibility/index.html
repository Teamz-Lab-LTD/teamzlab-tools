Accessibility Tools for Real-World Use

Accessibility is broader than checking color contrast or adding alt text. These tools cover practical day-to-day needs for disabled users, caregivers, teachers, developers, and designers. You can test how content may be experienced, build support documents, generate more inclusive materials, and review common accessibility requirements without needing specialized software.

For digital accessibility work, tools like the WCAG Accessibility Checklist, ADA Compliance Checker, Accessible Color Palette Generator, Color Blindness Simulator, and Screen Reader Text Preview help teams catch issues earlier in design or QA. For disability planning and neurodiversity support, the Spoon Theory Energy Planner, Visual Schedule Maker, IEP Goals Generator, and Autism Support Plan Generator are designed to be more immediately useful than a generic form or spreadsheet.

Built for Privacy and Clarity

Many of these pages handle sensitive information about health, disability, support needs, or education accommodations. Teamz Lab keeps the interaction lightweight and browser-based whenever possible so users can explore options privately. The goal is straightforward: make accessibility support easier to understand, easier to apply, and easier to share with the people who need it.
