What Is Your Take-Home Pay in Kenya?

Your take-home pay, or net salary, is the amount you actually receive in your bank account after all statutory deductions are removed from your gross salary. In Kenya, these deductions include PAYE (Pay As You Earn income tax), NSSF (National Social Security Fund), SHIF (Social Health Insurance Fund, which replaced NHIF), and the Affordable Housing Levy. Understanding the difference between gross and net salary is essential when budgeting, applying for loans, or negotiating job offers.

Many Kenyan employees are surprised by the gap between their offer letter gross salary and the actual amount deposited in their accounts. For a gross salary of KES 100,000, the net pay after all deductions is approximately KES 75,000, meaning about 25% goes to statutory deductions. For higher earners at KES 200,000 gross, the deduction percentage increases to approximately 30% due to higher PAYE rates. This calculator helps you quickly convert any gross salary to the expected net amount.

Kenya Net Pay Calculation

Net Pay: Gross − PAYE − NSSF − SHIF − Housing Levy

PAYE: Progressive tax on (Gross − NSSF) minus KES 2,400 relief

NSSF: 6% up to KES 36,000 ceiling

SHIF: 1.375% of Gross (employee share)

Housing Levy: 1.5% of Gross

Common Salary Ranges and Net Pay in Kenya

For reference, here are approximate net pay amounts for common gross salaries in Kenya: KES 50,000 gross yields approximately KES 40,000 net. KES 80,000 gross yields approximately KES 59,000 net. KES 100,000 gross yields approximately KES 75,000 net. KES 150,000 gross yields approximately KES 108,000 net. KES 200,000 gross yields approximately KES 140,000 net. These are estimates based on standard deductions and may vary if you have additional allowances, benefits, or deductions.

Factors That Can Affect Your Net Pay

Beyond the standard statutory deductions calculated here, several other factors can affect your actual net pay. Voluntary pension contributions above the mandatory NSSF reduce your taxable income. Mortgage interest relief (up to KES 25,000 per month) can reduce your PAYE. Insurance premiums for life or health insurance may also provide tax relief. On the other hand, salary advances, loan repayments, and other employer-specific deductions will further reduce your take-home pay. If your payslip shows deductions beyond what this calculator estimates, check with your HR department for details.
