How Much Does a Bathroom Remodel Cost?

Bathroom remodels range from $3,000 for a basic refresh to $30,000 or more for a luxury renovation. The average mid-range bathroom remodel costs $10,000-$20,000. A half bath costs significantly less than a full bathroom since there is no tub or shower work. Master bathroom renovations cost the most due to larger size and higher expectations for finishes. The biggest cost drivers are tile work, plumbing changes, and the vanity or shower enclosure choice.

Bathroom Renovation Cost Breakdown

Tile for floors and walls costs $1,000-$5,000 depending on material and area. A new vanity with sink and faucet runs $500-$4,000. Shower or tub installation ranges from $1,000 for a basic tub insert to $8,000 for a custom tile walk-in shower. Plumbing work costs $500-$3,000 depending on whether pipes need relocation. A new toilet costs $200-$800 installed. Electrical work for lighting, outlets, and exhaust fans runs $500-$2,000. Labor typically accounts for 40-50% of a bathroom remodel budget.

Bathroom Remodel Tips for Maximum Value

Focus on the vanity and shower as they create the biggest visual impact. Use large-format tiles to create a modern look with fewer grout lines and faster installation. Keep plumbing in the same locations to avoid expensive pipe relocation. Install a comfort-height toilet and proper ventilation for long-term satisfaction. Choose water-resistant materials for flooring since bathrooms get wet frequently. A frameless glass shower enclosure adds a luxury feel at moderate cost. Add adequate lighting including task lighting at the vanity and ambient overhead lighting.
