How Much Does a Home Renovation Cost?

Home renovation costs depend on the scope, quality of materials, and your location. A minor kitchen refresh costs $10,000-$25,000 while a major kitchen remodel runs $30,000-$75,000. Bathroom renovations range from $5,000-$25,000. Basement finishing costs $20,000-$50,000. Whole-house renovations typically cost $40-$75 per square foot. Labor accounts for 30-40% of total project costs. Always add 10-20% contingency for unexpected issues discovered during demolition.

Renovation Projects with Best ROI

Kitchen and bathroom renovations consistently offer the best return on investment, recouping 60-80% of costs at resale. Minor kitchen remodels have higher ROI than major ones. Adding a bathroom to a home with only one adds significant value. Exterior improvements like siding, roofing, and garage doors also have excellent ROI. Basement finishing adds livable square footage at a lower cost per square foot than building an addition. Energy efficiency upgrades like windows and insulation attract buyers and reduce operating costs.

Tips for Managing Renovation Costs

Get detailed written quotes from at least three contractors before committing. Establish a clear scope of work to avoid costly change orders. Choose mid-range materials that offer quality without premium pricing. Consider doing demolition yourself to reduce labor costs. Time your renovation during off-peak seasons for better contractor availability and pricing. Phase large projects to spread costs over time. Keep a detailed budget spreadsheet and track every expense against your original estimate.
