How Much Does a Kitchen Remodel Cost?

Kitchen remodel costs range from $10,000 for a minor refresh to $75,000 or more for a major upscale renovation. The average mid-range kitchen remodel costs $25,000-$40,000. Cabinets typically consume the largest share at 30-35% of the budget, followed by labor at 20-25%, countertops at 10-15%, and appliances at 10-15%. The remaining budget covers flooring, lighting, plumbing, electrical, and finishing touches. Kitchen size, layout changes, and material quality are the primary cost drivers.

Kitchen Remodel Cost Breakdown

Cabinets range from $3,000 for refacing to $20,000 or more for custom cabinetry. Countertops vary from $1,500 for laminate to $8,000 or more for natural stone like granite or quartz. Appliance packages range from $2,000 for basic models to $15,000 for premium brands. Flooring costs $1,000-$5,000 depending on material and kitchen size. Plumbing for a new sink and faucet runs $500-$2,500. Electrical work for new outlets, lighting, and circuits costs $1,000-$4,000. Painting, backsplash, and hardware add $500-$3,000.

Kitchen Renovation Tips to Save Money

Reface or paint existing cabinets instead of replacing them to save 50-70% on cabinet costs. Keep plumbing and gas lines in their current locations to avoid expensive relocation. Choose quartz or butcher block countertops instead of marble for a high-end look at lower cost. Buy appliances during holiday sales for 20-40% savings. Consider open shelving to replace some upper cabinets. Do demolition yourself to save on labor costs. Maintain the existing kitchen layout to avoid moving plumbing and electrical connections which add thousands to the budget.
