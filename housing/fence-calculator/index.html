How Much Does a Fence Cost?

Fence costs vary widely by material, height, and length. A standard 150 linear foot wood privacy fence costs $2,500-$5,000 installed. Chain-link is the most affordable at $10-20 per linear foot. Vinyl fencing offers low maintenance at $20-35 per linear foot. Composite fencing provides the look of wood without the upkeep at $25-45 per linear foot. Posts are typically set 6-8 feet apart and account for 20-30% of material costs. Always get at least three quotes from local contractors.

Fence Material Comparison

Wood fencing is the most popular choice offering versatility and a natural look but requires staining every 2-3 years. Cedar and redwood resist rot naturally but cost more than pine or spruce. Vinyl fencing never needs painting and lasts 20-30 years but has higher upfront costs. Chain-link is the most economical and durable but offers minimal privacy unless slats are added. Composite fencing combines recycled wood and plastic for a durable, low-maintenance option. Metal fencing like aluminum or wrought iron is decorative but offers minimal privacy.

Fence Installation Tips

Check local zoning laws and HOA rules before building since setback requirements and height restrictions vary. Call 811 to have utility lines marked before digging post holes. Set posts in concrete for maximum stability, especially in windy areas. Consider slope and drainage patterns when planning your fence line. Gates should be placed for convenient access and typically cost $100-500 each depending on size and material. Get a property survey to ensure you build on your own land and avoid disputes with neighbors.
