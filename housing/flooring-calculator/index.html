How to Calculate Flooring Needs

Measure your room length and width in feet to get the total square footage. Add 10-15% for waste due to cuts, mistakes, and pattern matching. For diagonal installations or complex room shapes, add 15-20% waste. This calculator factors in waste automatically so you order enough material the first time. Always round up to the nearest full box or carton when purchasing since partial boxes are usually not sold.

Flooring Material Costs Compared

Laminate flooring is the most affordable at $2-5 per square foot installed. Vinyl plank offers water resistance at $3-7 per square foot. Hardwood ranges from $6-15 per square foot depending on species and quality. Ceramic tile costs $5-10 per square foot including installation. Carpet is $3-8 per square foot installed. Premium options like engineered hardwood or natural stone can exceed $15-25 per square foot. Material costs typically represent 40-60% of total project cost with labor making up the rest.

DIY vs Professional Flooring Installation

Click-lock laminate and vinyl plank are the easiest for DIY installation, saving $2-5 per square foot in labor costs. Tile requires specialized tools and skills for proper installation. Hardwood installation is best left to professionals for a quality finish. When doing it yourself, rent tools from your local hardware store rather than buying them. Professional installation typically adds $2-8 per square foot but comes with warranties and ensures proper subfloor preparation.
