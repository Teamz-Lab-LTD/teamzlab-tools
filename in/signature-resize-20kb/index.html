Why Government Forms Need Signature Images Under 20KB

Indian government recruitment portals, competitive examination applications, and official form submission websites require applicants to upload a digital copy of their handwritten signature in a specific format and file size. The most common requirement across IBPS, SSC, RRB, and other major recruitment bodies is a signature image in JPEG format with a file size between 10KB and 20KB and dimensions around 140x60 pixels to 300x80 pixels. These strict specifications ensure uniform presentation across millions of applications, efficient server storage, and fast processing times during the verification stage. Scanned signatures that are too large, too high resolution, or in the wrong format will be rejected by the upload system, potentially preventing candidates from completing their applications before the deadline.

This tool is specifically designed for Indian competitive exam applicants and government job seekers who need to prepare their signature images quickly and accurately. It handles the dual challenge of resizing the image to standard dimensions of 300x80 pixels maximum while simultaneously compressing the file to fit within the 20KB limit. The processing happens entirely in your browser, so your personal signature, which is a sensitive biometric identifier, is never uploaded to any third-party server. This privacy protection is especially important for signature images that could potentially be misused for forgery if intercepted.

Signature Requirements by Examination Portal

IBPS (Institute of Banking Personnel Selection) requires signature images for bank PO, clerk, and specialist officer applications in JPEG format with a file size between 10KB and 20KB. The recommended dimensions are approximately 140x60 pixels for some cycles and up to 300x80 pixels for others. SSC (Staff Selection Commission) specifies signature images for CGL, CHSL, MTS, and other exams in JPEG format with a maximum size of 12KB to 20KB depending on the examination. RRB (Railway Recruitment Board) typically requires signature images under 20KB in JPEG format for NTPC, Group D, ALP, and JE applications. UPSC (Union Public Service Commission) may require signature images with slightly different specifications, generally under 20KB for most applications. State PSCs including MPSC, UPPSC, BPSC, WBPSC, RPSC, and others have similar requirements, usually specifying JPEG format under 20KB.

How to Scan Your Signature for Best Results

For the best quality signature image, sign with a dark blue or black ink pen on clean white paper. Use a medium to thick pen for better visibility at small sizes. Sign naturally within a space of about 4cm wide by 1.5cm tall. Scan the signature at 200 to 300 DPI using a flatbed scanner, or photograph it with your smartphone camera in good lighting. When photographing, hold the camera directly above the signature, ensure even lighting without shadows, and include only the signature area with minimal white space around it. Crop the image tightly around the signature before uploading to this tool. A clean, high-contrast signature with minimal background noise will produce the best results after compression.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid signing with a light-colored pen or pencil, as the signature becomes illegible after compression to 20KB. Do not scan or photograph the signature at extremely high resolution like 600 DPI or above, as this creates unnecessarily large files that require more aggressive compression. Avoid including too much white space around the signature, as this wastes pixels on empty area. Do not upload a photograph of the entire page with the signature somewhere on it, as the tool will resize the entire page and the signature will be tiny and unreadable. Make sure the paper is flat and wrinkle-free when scanning, and avoid shadows or uneven lighting when photographing. If your signature has very thin strokes, consider signing with a slightly thicker pen for better visibility at small sizes.
